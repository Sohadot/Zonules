Accommodation Reflex

The accommodation reflex is a coordinated accommodation mechanism

Definition

The accommodation reflex is the coordinated response of the visual system to bring a near object into focus, comprising lens accommodation, pupillary constriction, and convergence.

Scientific Grounding

The three components of the near triad — accommodation, miosis, and convergence — are neurally linked and activated together by the Edinger-Westphal nucleus. Age-related hardening of the crystalline lens reduces accommodative amplitude, producing presbyopia — the progressive loss of near-focusing range.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

The accommodation reflex is a coordinated accommodation mechanism (FIO-02): it reconfigures the optical system to extend its sharp-focus range to near distances on demand.
