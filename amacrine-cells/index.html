Amacrine Cells

Interneurons that tune the retina's output in time.

Definition

Amacrine cells are retinal interneurons that modulate the signal passing from bipolar cells to ganglion cells.

Scientific Grounding

Many amacrine cell types are specialized; some are essential for detecting the direction of motion. They shape the temporal dynamics of the retinal output, adapting it to changing conditions.

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Amacrine cells are an accommodation-layer mechanism within the retina: they adjust the signal so the output stays useful as conditions change.
