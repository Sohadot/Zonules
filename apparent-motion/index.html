Apparent Motion

Motion the system constructs where none physically occurs.

Definition

Apparent motion is the perception of movement produced by stationary stimuli presented in rapid succession.

Scientific Grounding

It underlies the perception of continuous motion in film and electronic displays. The phenomenon depends on the spatial and temporal separation between the successive stimuli.

*Sources: SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Apparent motion is an interpretation (FIO-05): motion is constructed by the visual system where none physically occurs.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Apparent Motion is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-05 — Interpretation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
