Bayesian Perception

Meaning as the most probable cause of the signal.

Definition

Bayesian perception models the percept as the most probable interpretation of sensory data given prior expectations.

Scientific Grounding

It combines incoming evidence with prior probabilities, weighting each by its reliability. The framework explains why ambiguous or noisy input is resolved toward expected interpretations.

*Sources: SRC-006, SRC-020. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Bayesian perception is an interpretation-layer rule: meaning is the most probable cause of the signal, not the signal itself.
