Binocular Disparity

Two eyes, two slightly different views, one sense of depth.

Definition

Binocular disparity is the small difference between the images of a scene formed in the two eyes because of their horizontal separation.

Scientific Grounding

The visual system measures this disparity and uses it to compute relative depth, the basis of stereopsis. Disparity is most informative for objects within a few meters, where the angular difference between the eyes is largest.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Binocular disparity is interpretation-layer evidence — two slightly different signals fused into one depth judgment.
