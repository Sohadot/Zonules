Binocular Rivalry

When two signals cannot be reconciled, one is read.

Definition

Binocular rivalry is the alternation in perception that occurs when the two eyes view incompatible images.

Scientific Grounding

Rather than blending, awareness switches between the two images over time. Rivalry is studied as a window into how the brain selects a single conscious percept.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Binocular rivalry is a separation-layer conflict: when two signals cannot be reconciled, only one is read at a time.
