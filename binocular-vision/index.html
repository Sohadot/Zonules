Binocular Vision

Depth is not seen; it is constructed from the small disagreement between two eyes.

Definition

Binocular vision integrates input from two laterally separated eyes into a single percept; binocular disparity is the primary cue for stereoscopic depth at close range.

Scientific Grounding

Binocular fusion requires the oculomotor system to maintain alignment of both eyes on the same point; misalignment produces diplopia or suppression of one eye's input. Binocular rivalry results in perceptual alternation between incompatible images from the two eyes, demonstrating active perceptual selection.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Binocular vision is an interpretation problem (FIO-05): the system must construct a depth representation from the disparity between two signals.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Binocular Vision is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-05 (Interpretation) concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
