Biological Motion

A handful of moving dots, instantly seen as a person.

Definition

Biological motion is the perception of a living, moving figure from only a few moving points, such as lights attached to the joints.

Scientific Grounding

Observers rapidly recognize walking, running, and even the identity or mood of a point-light figure from motion alone. The percept collapses when the points are static, showing that the information is carried by the pattern of movement.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-015. See the source registry for classification.*
