Bipolar Cells

The relay that carries light's signal inward.

Definition

Bipolar cells are the retinal interneurons that carry signals from photoreceptors to retinal ganglion cells.

Scientific Grounding

They split the visual signal into ON and OFF pathways that respond to increases and decreases in light. Bipolar cells contribute the center to the center-surround receptive fields of ganglion cells.

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-011. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Bipolar cells are a suspension-layer relay: they hold and route the photoreceptor signal into the channels the brain reads.
