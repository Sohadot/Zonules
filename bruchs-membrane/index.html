Bruch's Membrane

A thin sheet that keeps the outer retina supplied.

Definition

Bruch's membrane is a thin acellular layer between the retinal pigment epithelium and the choroid.

Scientific Grounding

It acts as a semipermeable barrier through which nutrients and waste pass between the choroidal blood supply and the outer retina. The membrane thickens and accumulates deposits with age, which can impede exchange across it.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Bruch's membrane is a maintenance element of the suspension layer: it holds the exchange conditions that keep the photoreceptors supplied and functional.
