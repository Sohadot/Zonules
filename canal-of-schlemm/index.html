Canal of Schlemm

The drain that holds the eye's internal pressure steady.

Definition

The canal of Schlemm is a circular channel at the iridocorneal angle that collects aqueous humor after it passes through the trabecular meshwork.

Scientific Grounding

It returns the aqueous humor to the venous circulation, completing the fluid's drainage pathway. The balance between aqueous production and outflow through this canal sets the intraocular pressure that maintains ocular shape.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Schlemm's canal is a maintenance element of the suspension layer: stable outflow holds the pressure that keeps the optical geometry intact.
