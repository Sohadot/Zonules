Chromatic Aberration

Color, the carrier of signal, also disperses it.

Definition

Chromatic aberration arises because the refractive index of the eye's media varies with wavelength, so different colors focus at slightly different planes.

Scientific Grounding

The eye focuses long wavelengths slightly behind short wavelengths, producing longitudinal chromatic aberration. The visual system partly compensates for chromatic aberration in its neural processing.

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Chromatic aberration is a separation-layer artifact: the same dispersion that carries color also blurs the image across wavelength.
