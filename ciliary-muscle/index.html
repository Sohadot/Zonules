Ciliary Muscle

The hidden muscle that changes the range of focus.

Definition

The ciliary muscle is a ring of smooth muscle within the ciliary body whose contraction governs the tension carried by the zonular fibers.

Scientific Grounding

When the ciliary muscle contracts, zonular tension falls and the elastic lens rounds up for near focus; when it relaxes, zonular tension rises and the lens flattens for distance. The muscle is driven by parasympathetic fibers traveling with the oculomotor nerve.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

The ciliary muscle is the actuator of the accommodation layer — the structure that changes the operating range of focus.
