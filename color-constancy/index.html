Color Constancy

Color constancy is an interpretation

Definition

Color constancy is the ability of the visual system to perceive the surface color of objects as stable despite changes in the spectral composition of the illuminating light.

Scientific Grounding

The visual system achieves color constancy through comparisons across the visual field and by estimating and discounting the illuminant. Color constancy is not perfect; under extreme spectral shifts the same surface can appear to have different colors, as illustrated by well-known optical illusions.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Color constancy is an interpretation (FIO-05): perceived surface color is a stable output constructed by the visual system rather than a direct read-out of the wavelength distribution reaching the eye.
