Complex Cells

Oriented features, freed from exact position.

Definition

Complex cells are visual cortex neurons that respond to an oriented edge anywhere within their receptive field.

Scientific Grounding

Unlike simple cells, they are largely insensitive to the exact position of the stimulus. Many complex cells are also direction-selective for motion.

*Sources: SRC-010, SRC-009. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Complex cells are a separation-layer abstraction: they report that an oriented feature is present without pinning it to one location.
