Cone Cells

The daylight photoreceptors that resolve detail and color.

Definition

Cone cells are the photoreceptors responsible for vision in bright light and for color.

Scientific Grounding

Human retinas contain three cone types tuned to short, medium, and long wavelengths. Cones are densely packed in the fovea, where they support the sharpest vision.

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Cones are the separation layer's daytime instrument: they resolve fine detail and color when signal is plentiful.
