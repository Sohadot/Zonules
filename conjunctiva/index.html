Conjunctiva

The protective membrane that keeps the optical surface clear.

Definition

The conjunctiva is the thin transparent mucous membrane covering the sclera and lining the inner eyelids.

Scientific Grounding

It contributes to the tear film and protects the ocular surface from debris and infection. Inflammation of the conjunctiva, called conjunctivitis, is a common ocular surface condition.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

The conjunctiva is a signal-noise boundary (FIO-03): it keeps the ocular surface clear so the optical path stays uncorrupted.
