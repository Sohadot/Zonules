# Medical Disclaimer

> This is an educational reference. It is not a clinical tool and it does not give advice about your eyes.

## Scope of this asset

Zonules.com is a governed educational reference system. It publishes reference units on the anatomy of the eye, the science of visual perception, and the architecture of machine vision systems. Its purpose is to explain how focus works and how it fails — conceptually, anatomically, and in machines.

## What this asset is not

Zonules.com is not a medical resource, a clinical tool, or a source of health advice. Nothing on this asset:

- Diagnoses any condition.
- Assesses or describes the state of any individual's eyes or vision.
- Recommends treatment, intervention, or any clinical course of action.
- Replaces or supplements a consultation with a qualified eye-care professional.

If you have questions about your eyes or vision, consult a qualified eye-care professional. Nothing on this asset should influence a decision about your health.

## Anatomical and physiological content

Reference units on Layer 01 (Anatomy) describe the structure and function of the eye as documented in standard academic and institutional sources. They are educational descriptions of how the eye is built and how it works in general terms — they are not descriptions of any particular person's anatomy, and they are not intended to help anyone assess their own visual function.

These units carry a medical-educational safety classification. Every such unit contains an explicit Safety Notes section stating these limits in place, so the framing appears where the content does.

## Claims and sourcing

Anatomical and physiological claims are sourced to registered academic textbooks and institutional educational materials. They are not clinical claims and they are not intended to support clinical decisions. The sourcing classification is documented on each page and in the [source policy](/source-policy/).

## Accuracy and updates

This asset aims for accuracy against registered academic sources. It does not claim completeness, and it does not substitute for current clinical knowledge or institutional guidance. Reference units are reviewed on a governance cycle; the `last_reviewed` date on each unit shows when it was last checked.
