# Source Policy

> The difference between a reference and an opinion is provenance. This policy defines the provenance.

## The rule

Every statement on Zonules.com that asserts a factual relationship about anatomy, physiology, optics, cognition, machine systems, or verification must be one of the following: supported by a registered source, marked as conceptual interpretation, marked as internal framework language, or excluded from publication. No unsourced scientific claim is published, and no conceptual metaphor is allowed to impersonate evidence.

## Source classification

Registered sources are classified by type so that a reader can weigh them appropriately:

- Peer-reviewed paper
- Medical institution
- Academic textbook or reference
- Standards body
- Government or regulatory source
- Technical documentation
- Conceptual or internal framework

Conceptual and internal-framework sources are never treated as external fact. They mark where the asset is making an interpretive mapping — for example, extending the anatomy of the zonules into a general model of focus integrity — and they are labelled as such on every page.

## Claim classification

Each registered claim carries a status:

- Sourced — rests on one or more registered external references
- Framework — an internal conceptual mapping, explicitly not external fact
- Conceptual — an interpretation marked as such

The References section on every reference unit lists its claims with these labels and the supporting citations, so the basis of each statement is visible in place.

## What this protects against

This policy makes three failures impossible to publish quietly: an unsourced scientific assertion presented as fact, a speculative statement dressed as established knowledge, and a conceptual metaphor borrowing the authority of evidence. The quality gate enforces the policy automatically; a page whose claims do not resolve to registered sources cannot be published.

## Medical safety

Anatomical and physiological reference content is educational only. It is not diagnosis, treatment, or medical advice, and it is not a substitute for a qualified eye-care professional. Source-backed anatomical claims are kept distinct from any clinical interpretation, and reference units carry the relevant medical-safety framing.
