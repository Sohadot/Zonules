# Afterimage

> An impression that outlasts its cause.

## Definition

An afterimage is a visual impression that persists or appears after the original stimulus is removed. [CLM-984]

## Scientific Grounding

Negative afterimages, in complementary colors, follow prolonged fixation and reflect adaptation of photoreceptors and neurons. [CLM-985] Afterimages move with the eyes because they originate in the retina. [CLM-986]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

An afterimage is an accommodation-layer cost: the system's adjustment to one stimulus distorts the next percept. [CLM-987]
