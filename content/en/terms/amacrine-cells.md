# Amacrine Cells

> Interneurons that tune the retina's output in time.

## Definition

Amacrine cells are retinal interneurons that modulate the signal passing from bipolar cells to ganglion cells. [CLM-636]

## Scientific Grounding

Many amacrine cell types are specialized; some are essential for detecting the direction of motion. [CLM-637] They shape the temporal dynamics of the retinal output, adapting it to changing conditions. [CLM-638]

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Amacrine cells are an accommodation-layer mechanism within the retina: they adjust the signal so the output stays useful as conditions change. [CLM-639]
