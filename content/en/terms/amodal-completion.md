# Amodal Completion

> The hidden half of a thing is seen as still there.

## Definition

Amodal completion is the perception of a whole object even when part of it is hidden behind an occluder. [CLM-692]

## Scientific Grounding

The visual system represents the occluded portion as present and continuous though it is not in the image. [CLM-693] This filling-in follows regularities such as the good continuation of the visible contours. [CLM-694]

*Sources: SRC-006, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Amodal completion is an interpretation-layer inference — the percept asserts structure the signal does not directly contain. [CLM-695]

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Amodal Completion is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the [focus-integrity ontology](/focus-integrity-ontology/) is fixed as an FIO-05 — Interpretation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
