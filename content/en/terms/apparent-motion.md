# Apparent Motion

> Motion the system constructs where none physically occurs.

## Definition

Apparent motion is the perception of movement produced by stationary stimuli presented in rapid succession. [CLM-292]

## Scientific Grounding

It underlies the perception of continuous motion in film and electronic displays. [CLM-293] The phenomenon depends on the spatial and temporal separation between the successive stimuli. [CLM-294]

*Sources: SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Apparent motion is an interpretation (FIO-05): motion is constructed by the visual system where none physically occurs. [CLM-295]

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Apparent Motion is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result.
