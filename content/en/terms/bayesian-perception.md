# Bayesian Perception

> Meaning as the most probable cause of the signal.

## Definition

Bayesian perception models the percept as the most probable interpretation of sensory data given prior expectations. [CLM-956]

## Scientific Grounding

It combines incoming evidence with prior probabilities, weighting each by its reliability. [CLM-957] The framework explains why ambiguous or noisy input is resolved toward expected interpretations. [CLM-958]

*Sources: SRC-006, SRC-020. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Bayesian perception is an interpretation-layer rule: meaning is the most probable cause of the signal, not the signal itself. [CLM-959]
