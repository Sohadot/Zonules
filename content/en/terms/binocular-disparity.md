# Binocular Disparity

> Two eyes, two slightly different views, one sense of depth.

## Definition

Binocular disparity is the small difference between the images of a scene formed in the two eyes because of their horizontal separation. [CLM-680]

## Scientific Grounding

The visual system measures this disparity and uses it to compute relative depth, the basis of stereopsis. [CLM-681] Disparity is most informative for objects within a few meters, where the angular difference between the eyes is largest. [CLM-682]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Binocular disparity is interpretation-layer evidence — two slightly different signals fused into one depth judgment. [CLM-683]
