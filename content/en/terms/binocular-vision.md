# Binocular Vision

> Depth is not seen; it is constructed from the small disagreement between two eyes.

## Definition

Binocular vision integrates input from two laterally separated eyes into a single percept; binocular disparity is the primary cue for stereoscopic depth at close range. [CLM-146]

## Scientific Grounding

Binocular fusion requires the oculomotor system to maintain alignment of both eyes on the same point; misalignment produces diplopia or suppression of one eye's input. [CLM-147] Binocular rivalry results in perceptual alternation between incompatible images from the two eyes, demonstrating active perceptual selection. [CLM-148]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Binocular vision is an interpretation problem (FIO-05): the system must construct a depth representation from the disparity between two signals. [CLM-149]
