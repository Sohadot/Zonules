# Biological Motion

> A handful of moving dots, instantly seen as a person.

## Definition

Biological motion is the perception of a living, moving figure from only a few moving points, such as lights attached to the joints. [CLM-688]

## Scientific Grounding

Observers rapidly recognize walking, running, and even the identity or mood of a point-light figure from motion alone. [CLM-689] The percept collapses when the points are static, showing that the information is carried by the pattern of movement. [CLM-690]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-015. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Biological motion is an interpretation-layer achievement — meaning recovered from motion structure that carries no form on its own. [CLM-691]
