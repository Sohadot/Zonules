# Bipolar Cells

> The relay that carries light's signal inward.

## Definition

Bipolar cells are the retinal interneurons that carry signals from photoreceptors to retinal ganglion cells. [CLM-632]

## Scientific Grounding

They split the visual signal into ON and OFF pathways that respond to increases and decreases in light. [CLM-633] Bipolar cells contribute the center to the center-surround receptive fields of ganglion cells. [CLM-634]

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-011. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Bipolar cells are a suspension-layer relay: they hold and route the photoreceptor signal into the channels the brain reads. [CLM-635]
