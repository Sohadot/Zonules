# Bistable Perception

> One signal, two readings, alternating without the stimulus changing.

## Definition

Bistable perception occurs when a single unchanging stimulus alternates between two stable interpretations. [CLM-300]

## Scientific Grounding

Reversible figures and other ambiguous images are classic examples of bistable perception. [CLM-301] The competing interpretations alternate over time without any change in the stimulus itself. [CLM-302]

*Sources: SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Bistable perception is a signal-noise ambiguity (FIO-03): the same signal supports competing readings. [CLM-303]
