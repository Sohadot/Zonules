# Blind Spot

> A hole in the image the mind quietly fills with no signal behind it.

## Definition

The blind spot, or optic disc, is the region of the retina where the optic nerve exits and which contains no photoreceptors, producing a gap in the visual field. [CLM-228]

## Scientific Grounding

Because the optic disc lacks photoreceptors, no visual signal originates from that location. [CLM-229] The visual system normally fills in the blind spot so that the gap is not consciously perceived. [CLM-230]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The blind spot is a provenance gap (FIO-04): a region where the perceived image is constructed rather than recorded, with no underlying signal of origin. [CLM-231]
