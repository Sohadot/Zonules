# Bruch's Membrane

> A thin sheet that keeps the outer retina supplied.

## Definition

Bruch's membrane is a thin acellular layer between the retinal pigment epithelium and the choroid. [CLM-612]

## Scientific Grounding

It acts as a semipermeable barrier through which nutrients and waste pass between the choroidal blood supply and the outer retina. [CLM-613] The membrane thickens and accumulates deposits with age, which can impede exchange across it. [CLM-614]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Bruch's membrane is a maintenance element of the suspension layer: it holds the exchange conditions that keep the photoreceptors supplied and functional. [CLM-615]
