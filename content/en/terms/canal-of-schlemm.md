# Canal of Schlemm

> The drain that holds the eye's internal pressure steady.

## Definition

The canal of Schlemm is a circular channel at the iridocorneal angle that collects aqueous humor after it passes through the trabecular meshwork. [CLM-604]

## Scientific Grounding

It returns the aqueous humor to the venous circulation, completing the fluid's drainage pathway. [CLM-605] The balance between aqueous production and outflow through this canal sets the intraocular pressure that maintains ocular shape. [CLM-606]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Schlemm's canal is a maintenance element of the suspension layer: stable outflow holds the pressure that keeps the optical geometry intact. [CLM-607]
