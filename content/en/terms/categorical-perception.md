# Categorical Perception

> A smooth continuum experienced as distinct kinds.

## Definition

Categorical perception is the tendency to perceive continuously varying stimuli as belonging to discrete categories. [CLM-724]

## Scientific Grounding

Differences that cross a category boundary are perceived more readily than equally large differences within a category. [CLM-725] It appears in domains such as color and facial expression, where a smooth physical change is experienced as a set of distinct types. [CLM-726]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-015. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Categorical perception is an interpretation-layer commitment — the percept resolves a continuum into named kinds. [CLM-727]
