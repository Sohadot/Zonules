# Choroid

> The hidden vascular layer that keeps the photoreceptors alive so focus has something to land on.

## Definition

The choroid is the highly vascular layer between the sclera and retina, supplying oxygen and nutrients to the outer retinal layers including the photoreceptors and retinal pigment epithelium, which are avascular. [CLM-157]

## Scientific Grounding

The choriocapillaris beneath the macula has the highest blood flow per unit tissue of any structure in the body, reflecting the exceptional metabolic demands of macular photoreceptors. [CLM-158]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The choroid is a metabolic suspension element of focus integrity (FIO-01): it sustains the living infrastructure of phototransduction. [CLM-159]
