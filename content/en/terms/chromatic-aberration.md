# Chromatic Aberration

> Color, the carrier of signal, also disperses it.

## Definition

Chromatic aberration arises because the refractive index of the eye's media varies with wavelength, so different colors focus at slightly different planes. [CLM-872]

## Scientific Grounding

The eye focuses long wavelengths slightly behind short wavelengths, producing longitudinal chromatic aberration. [CLM-873] The visual system partly compensates for chromatic aberration in its neural processing. [CLM-874]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Chromatic aberration is a separation-layer artifact: the same dispersion that carries color also blurs the image across wavelength. [CLM-875]
