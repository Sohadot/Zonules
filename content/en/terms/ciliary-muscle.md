# Ciliary Muscle

> The hidden muscle that changes the range of focus.

## Definition

The ciliary muscle is a ring of smooth muscle within the ciliary body whose contraction governs the tension carried by the zonular fibers. [CLM-608]

## Scientific Grounding

When the ciliary muscle contracts, zonular tension falls and the elastic lens rounds up for near focus; when it relaxes, zonular tension rises and the lens flattens for distance. [CLM-609] The muscle is driven by parasympathetic fibers traveling with the oculomotor nerve. [CLM-610]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The ciliary muscle is the actuator of the accommodation layer — the structure that changes the operating range of focus. [CLM-611]
