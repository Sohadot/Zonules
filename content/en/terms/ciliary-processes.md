# Ciliary Processes

> Where the eye makes the fluid that holds its pressure.

## Definition

The ciliary processes are the folded structures of the ciliary body that secrete the aqueous humor. [CLM-860]

## Scientific Grounding

They contain a rich capillary network from which the aqueous humor is produced. [CLM-861] The rate of aqueous production here, balanced against drainage, helps set the intraocular pressure. [CLM-862]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The ciliary processes are a suspension-layer maintenance element: they generate the fluid that holds the eye's pressure and nourishment. [CLM-863]
