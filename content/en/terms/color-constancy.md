# Color Constancy

> Color constancy is an interpretation

## Definition

Color constancy is the ability of the visual system to perceive the surface color of objects as stable despite changes in the spectral composition of the illuminating light. [CLM-372]

## Scientific Grounding

The visual system achieves color constancy through comparisons across the visual field and by estimating and discounting the illuminant. [CLM-373] Color constancy is not perfect; under extreme spectral shifts the same surface can appear to have different colors, as illustrated by well-known optical illusions. [CLM-374]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Color constancy is an interpretation (FIO-05): perceived surface color is a stable output constructed by the visual system rather than a direct read-out of the wavelength distribution reaching the eye. [CLM-375]
