# Complex Cells

> Oriented features, freed from exact position.

## Definition

Complex cells are visual cortex neurons that respond to an oriented edge anywhere within their receptive field. [CLM-924]

## Scientific Grounding

Unlike simple cells, they are largely insensitive to the exact position of the stimulus. [CLM-925] Many complex cells are also direction-selective for motion. [CLM-926]

*Sources: SRC-010, SRC-009. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Complex cells are a separation-layer abstraction: they report that an oriented feature is present without pinning it to one location. [CLM-927]
