# Cone Cells

> The daylight photoreceptors that resolve detail and color.

## Definition

Cone cells are the photoreceptors responsible for vision in bright light and for color. [CLM-644]

## Scientific Grounding

Human retinas contain three cone types tuned to short, medium, and long wavelengths. [CLM-645] Cones are densely packed in the fovea, where they support the sharpest vision. [CLM-646]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Cones are the separation layer's daytime instrument: they resolve fine detail and color when signal is plentiful. [CLM-647]
