# Conjunctiva

> The protective membrane that keeps the optical surface clear.

## Definition

The conjunctiva is the thin transparent mucous membrane covering the sclera and lining the inner eyelids. [CLM-252]

## Scientific Grounding

It contributes to the tear film and protects the ocular surface from debris and infection. [CLM-253] Inflammation of the conjunctiva, called conjunctivitis, is a common ocular surface condition. [CLM-254]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The conjunctiva is a signal-noise boundary (FIO-03): it keeps the ocular surface clear so the optical path stays uncorrupted. [CLM-255]
