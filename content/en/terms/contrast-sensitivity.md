# Contrast Sensitivity

> Detail is useless if a figure cannot lift off its background.

## Definition

Contrast sensitivity is the visual system's ability to distinguish luminance differences between adjacent regions. [CLM-268]

## Scientific Grounding

It varies with spatial frequency, a relationship described by the contrast sensitivity function. [CLM-269] Contrast sensitivity can be reduced even when fine-detail acuity is preserved. [CLM-270]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Contrast sensitivity is a signal-noise capacity (FIO-03): the threshold at which a figure separates from its background. [CLM-271]
