# Contrast Threshold

> The contrast threshold is the signal-noise boundary of the visual system

## Definition

The contrast threshold is the minimum difference in luminance between a target and its background that is reliably detectable by the visual system. [CLM-504]

## Scientific Grounding

Threshold contrast depends on spatial frequency, background luminance, stimulus duration, and eccentricity; it is the fundamental limit of signal detection in vision. [CLM-505] Clinical measurement of contrast sensitivity is more sensitive to early retinal and neural pathology than measurement of high-contrast acuity alone. [CLM-506]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The contrast threshold is the signal-noise boundary of the visual system (FIO-03): it defines the minimum signal-to-noise ratio required for a stimulus to be resolved. [CLM-507]
