# Corneal Endothelium

> A pump that holds the cornea clear by keeping it dry.

## Definition

The corneal endothelium is a single layer of cells on the posterior surface of the cornea that pumps fluid out of the corneal stroma to keep it dehydrated and clear. [CLM-592]

## Scientific Grounding

Endothelial cells do not regenerate in significant numbers after birth, so their density declines across life while the remaining cells spread to maintain coverage. [CLM-593] When endothelial pump function is lost, the stroma swells, scatters light, and the cornea loses transparency. [CLM-594]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The endothelium is a suspension-layer maintenance element: it holds the optical clarity of the cornea by continuously removing the fluid that would otherwise blur it. [CLM-595]
