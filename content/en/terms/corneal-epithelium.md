# Corneal Epithelium

> The eye's renewing front surface, where the image first forms.

## Definition

The corneal epithelium is the outermost cellular layer of the cornea, a stratified non-keratinized surface that forms the eye's first refracting interface with air. [CLM-596]

## Scientific Grounding

It regenerates rapidly from limbal stem cells, so minor surface abrasions can heal within days. [CLM-597] The epithelium together with the overlying tear film creates the smooth optical surface responsible for most of the eye's refractive power. [CLM-598]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

As a renewing protective surface, the epithelium is a suspension-layer element: it maintains the boundary conditions under which a sharp image can form. [CLM-599]
