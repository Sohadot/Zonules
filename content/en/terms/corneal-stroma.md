# Corneal Stroma

> Transparency engineered into living tissue.

## Definition

The corneal stroma is the thick middle layer that makes up about ninety percent of the cornea's thickness. [CLM-852]

## Scientific Grounding

Its transparency depends on the precise, regular spacing of collagen fibrils, which minimizes the scattering of light. [CLM-853] When the stroma swells with fluid the regular spacing is disrupted and the cornea scatters light and clouds. [CLM-854]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The stroma is a separation-layer structure: its ordered architecture is what lets signal pass without being scattered into noise. [CLM-855]
