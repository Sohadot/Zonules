# Cortical Magnification

> The cortex spends its resolution where signal matters.

## Definition

Cortical magnification is the disproportionate amount of visual cortex devoted to the central visual field relative to the periphery. [CLM-908]

## Scientific Grounding

A tiny foveal region maps onto a large cortical area, mirroring the fovea's dense photoreceptor sampling. [CLM-909] This magnification underlies the steep fall of acuity from the center toward the periphery. [CLM-910]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Cortical magnification is a separation-layer allocation: the system spends its resolution where the signal matters most. [CLM-911]
