# Dark Adaptation

> Dark adaptation is an accommodation mechanism

## Definition

Dark adaptation is the process by which the visual system increases its sensitivity after exposure to bright conditions, driven by photoreceptor recovery. [CLM-360]

## Scientific Grounding

The process occurs in two phases: an initial rapid cone-mediated phase followed by a slower rod-mediated phase that achieves full sensitivity recovery. [CLM-361] Recovery of rod pigment rhodopsin, bleached by bright light, underlies the slow phase and determines its time course. [CLM-362]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Dark adaptation is an accommodation mechanism (FIO-02): the optical system dynamically extends its operating range to recover signal in low-luminance environments. [CLM-363]
