# Depth Cues

> The evidence from which a flat image becomes a world.

## Definition

Depth cues are the sources of information the visual system uses to recover three-dimensional structure from two-dimensional retinal images. [CLM-676]

## Scientific Grounding

They are commonly divided into binocular cues, such as disparity, and monocular cues, such as occlusion, perspective, and shading. [CLM-677] No single cue is sufficient in all conditions, so the visual system combines the available cues to estimate depth. [CLM-678]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Depth cues are interpretation-layer evidence — the inputs from which the percept of a third dimension is constructed. [CLM-679]
