# Divided Attention

> Two tasks at once, and focus spread too thin for both.

## Definition

Divided attention is the attempt to process two or more sources or tasks at the same time. [CLM-700]

## Scientific Grounding

Performance typically declines as tasks compete for limited capacity, especially when they draw on the same resources. [CLM-701] Some highly practiced combinations can be performed together with little cost, while novel or similar tasks interfere strongly. [CLM-702]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Divided attention is a suspension-layer stress test — it reveals the limits of how much focus can be held at once. [CLM-703]
