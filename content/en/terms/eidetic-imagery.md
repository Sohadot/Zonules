# Eidetic Imagery

> A percept treated as if its signal had not ended.

## Definition

Eidetic imagery is the rare ability to retain a vivid, detailed visual image of a scene for an extended period after it is removed. [CLM-948]

## Scientific Grounding

It is most often reported in a small minority of children and is uncommon in adults. [CLM-949] True eidetic images are scanned as if still present, distinguishing them from ordinary memory. [CLM-950]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*
