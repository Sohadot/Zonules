# Expectation Suppression

> The predictable carries little signal.

## Definition

Expectation suppression is the reduced neural response to stimuli that are expected compared with unexpected ones. [CLM-968]

## Scientific Grounding

It is consistent with the idea that the brain encodes what was not predicted rather than the full input. [CLM-969] The effect appears across many levels of the visual hierarchy. [CLM-970]

*Sources: SRC-021, SRC-020. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Expectation suppression is an interpretation-layer economy: the predictable carries little signal, so the system spends less on it. [CLM-971]
