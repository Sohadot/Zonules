# Extraocular Muscles

> The actuators that aim the eye wherever attention demands.

## Definition

The extraocular muscles are the six muscles that rotate each eye within the orbit to direct gaze. [CLM-240]

## Scientific Grounding

They comprise four rectus and two oblique muscles, each producing characteristic eye rotations. [CLM-241] Coordinated action of the extraocular muscles of both eyes maintains binocular alignment. [CLM-242]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The extraocular muscles are the actuators of the accommodation-of-gaze system (FIO-02): the mechanism that physically re-aims focus. [CLM-243]
