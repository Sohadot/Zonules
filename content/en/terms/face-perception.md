# Face Perception

> Face perception is a high-priority interpretation system

## Definition

Face perception is the visual processing of facial information, including identity, emotional expression, gaze direction, and age, supported by dedicated neural mechanisms. [CLM-388]

## Scientific Grounding

The fusiform face area and related regions respond preferentially to faces; face processing is holistic and relies on the configuration of internal features relative to the face outline. [CLM-389] Faces are the visual category most resistant to recognition failure and most susceptible to systematic perceptual illusions, including the hollow-face illusion. [CLM-390]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Face perception is a high-priority interpretation system (FIO-05): the visual system reads identity, intent, and state from a two-dimensional signal — outputs that are absent from the signal itself. [CLM-391]
