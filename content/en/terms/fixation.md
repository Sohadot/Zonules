# Ocular Fixation

> Holding still is not stillness—it is active stabilization.

## Definition

Ocular fixation is the maintenance of gaze on a single location, holding the target's image on the fovea. [CLM-260]

## Scientific Grounding

Even during fixation the eyes make tiny involuntary movements: microsaccades, drift, and tremor. [CLM-261] These fixational eye movements prevent the perceptual fading of a stabilized image. [CLM-262]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Fixation is the ocular suspension of focus (FIO-01): active stabilization that holds the focal target in place. [CLM-263]
