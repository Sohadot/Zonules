# Fovea Centralis

> Every optical element in the eye exists to deliver one clean signal to this single point.

## Definition

The fovea centralis is a shallow depression at the centre of the macula, approximately 1.5 mm in diameter, containing the highest concentration of cone photoreceptors in the eye. [CLM-133]

## Scientific Grounding

At the foveal centre, overlying retinal layers are displaced laterally, leaving only cone photoreceptors to receive the focused image with minimal optical scatter. [CLM-134] The fovea contains no rod photoreceptors; it is entirely cone-mediated, conferring high-acuity, colour-sensitive vision in the central visual field. [CLM-135]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The fovea is the anatomical zero-point of focus integrity (FIO-03): every optical element exists to deliver a clean signal to this location. [CLM-136]
