# Foveola

> The retina's sharpest point, a floor of cones only.

## Definition

The foveola is the central floor of the fovea, roughly a third of a millimeter across, containing only cone photoreceptors and no rods. [CLM-616]

## Scientific Grounding

At the foveola the overlying retinal layers are displaced aside, giving incoming light a nearly unobstructed path to the cones. [CLM-617] This is the region of highest spatial acuity in the human retina. [CLM-618]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The foveola is the separation layer's sharpest instrument — the anatomical point where signal is resolved from noise at the finest scale. [CLM-619]
