# Geometric Illusions

> Rules that recover real structure can misfire.

## Definition

Geometric illusions are systematic misperceptions of the length, size, or angle of figures. [CLM-1008]

## Scientific Grounding

In the Müller-Lyer illusion, identical lines appear to differ in length because of their end decorations. [CLM-1009] Many such illusions are linked to mechanisms that normally aid depth and size interpretation. [CLM-1010]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Geometric illusions are an interpretation-layer misfire: rules that usually recover real structure produce confident error. [CLM-1011]
