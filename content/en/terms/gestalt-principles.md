# Gestalt Principles

> Before you can focus on a thing, the perceptual system must decide that it is a thing. The Gestalt principles are the rules of that decision.

## Definition

The **Gestalt principles** are a set of rules describing how the visual system organizes individual elements into perceptual wholes. The core grouping laws are **proximity** (elements close to each other tend to be grouped), **similarity** (elements that resemble each other tend to be grouped), **continuity** (elements that form a smooth path tend to be grouped), **closure** (gaps in a shape are filled in to produce a complete form), and **figure-ground** (any visual scene is organized into a figure that stands against a ground). [CLM-063]

The principles were described by Gestalt psychologists in the early twentieth century and have since been integrated into computational and cognitive accounts of vision.

## Scientific Grounding

Gestalt organization reflects fundamental tendencies of the perceptual system to structure visual input into coherent units — tendencies that do not reduce to learning from individual experience alone but appear to reflect deeper principles of how the visual system resolves ambiguity. [CLM-064]

These tendencies matter because vision is always ambiguous at the level of raw signal: an array of oriented edges can be organized into dozens of different objects depending on which elements are grouped. The Gestalt principles are the default resolution strategies the visual system applies when the input alone does not determine the answer.

When these organizational strategies fail — in certain visual disorders, or with the pathologically ambiguous figures studied in perceptual science — the percept becomes unstable, oscillating between incompatible organizations. [CLM-065] No single interpretation can be held.

*Sources: sensation and perception and vision science references (SRC-005, SRC-006). See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Grammar of Signal Separation

The Gestalt principles are the structural grammar of perceptual signal separation — the level at which the visual system determines what counts as signal and what counts as noise before individual elements can be interpreted. [CLM-066] They operate before constancy, before attention, before identification. They are the condition for there being a figure to attend to.

This is why they map to FIO-03 (Signal–Noise Failure): Gestalt failure is not a failure to interpret a recognized object but a failure to separate the field into organized units in the first place. The system has received input, but signal has not yet been distinguished from noise.

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

The governing thesis connects focus to hidden structural tension. The Gestalt principles are the structural rules that make it possible to have a figure to focus on. They are not what focusing is; they are what makes focusing possible. This is the perceptual echo of the zonular thesis: the structure that produces the object is not part of the object.

## Relationship to Machine Vision (Layer 03)

In machine vision, the analogue of Gestalt organization is the process by which the model groups pixels into objects and regions — segmentation, clustering, and feature grouping. A model that cannot perform stable grouping exhibits signal-noise failure at the machine level. This is a conceptual mapping, marked as internal framework language.
