# Horizontal Cells

> Lateral wiring that sharpens edges in the retina.

## Definition

Horizontal cells are retinal interneurons that connect photoreceptors laterally and feed inhibitory signals back to them. [CLM-628]

## Scientific Grounding

This lateral inhibition enhances contrast at edges by suppressing responses in uniformly lit regions. [CLM-629] Horizontal cells help build the center-surround organization of downstream receptive fields. [CLM-630]

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-011. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Horizontal cells are a separation-layer mechanism: they sharpen the boundary between signal and background before the image leaves the retina. [CLM-631]
