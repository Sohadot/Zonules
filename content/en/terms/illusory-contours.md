# Illusory Contours

> An edge seen clearly where no edge exists.

## Definition

Illusory contours are perceived edges that appear where no luminance boundary exists in the image, as in the Kanizsa triangle. [CLM-728]

## Scientific Grounding

The visual system infers a continuous edge and an occluding surface from the arrangement of surrounding inducers. [CLM-729] The illusory surface is often seen as brighter than its physically identical background. [CLM-730]

*Sources: SRC-006, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Illusory contours are an interpretation-layer construction — a boundary asserted by the percept that the signal does not contain. [CLM-731]
