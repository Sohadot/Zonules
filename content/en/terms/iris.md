# Iris

> The lens focuses. The cornea refracts. The iris decides how much signal is allowed to enter at all.

## Definition

The **iris** is the pigmented, muscular, circular diaphragm that sits in front of the [crystalline lens](/crystalline-lens/) and gives the eye its visible color. [CLM-075] Its central opening is the **pupil** — the aperture through which light passes into the eye. By varying the size of the pupil, the iris regulates the amount of light that reaches the retina.

## Scientific Grounding

The iris contains two antagonistic sets of smooth muscle: the **sphincter pupillae**, arranged circularly, which constricts the pupil in bright conditions, and the **dilator pupillae**, arranged radially, which opens it in dim conditions. [CLM-076] This antagonistic arrangement allows the pupil to operate across several orders of magnitude of light intensity, from a few millimeters in full daylight to eight or more millimeters in darkness.

The response is primarily mediated by the autonomic nervous system and operates continuously and without conscious effort.

The National Eye Institute describes the iris as the colored part of the eye that adjusts the size of the pupil to control how much light reaches the retina. [CLM-833]

*Sources: ocular basic science and anatomical references (SRC-001, SRC-002, SRC-025). See the source registry for classification.*

## The Accommodation of Input

The iris regulates not what is focused, but what is allowed in. This places it in the accommodation layer of the optical system: it adjusts the range of incoming signal to match the conditions. [CLM-077] When the iris cannot respond — as in pharmacological dilation or autonomic dysfunction — the range of usable signal narrows: in bright light the retina is flooded; in dim light it is starved.

This is an accommodation failure, not a focusing failure. The optical elements downstream may be perfectly functional, but without the iris's regulation, the signal they receive is outside the optimal operating range.

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

The focus-integrity thesis holds that focus is produced by hidden structural tension. The iris contributes to this by operating invisibly: the viewer does not experience pupil dilation as an act of seeing; they simply see more or less clearly. The aperture adjustment is a background structural process, exactly like the zonular tension that holds the lens.

## Relationship to Perception (Layer 02) and Machine Vision (Layer 03)

In perception, the analogue of the iris is attentional capacity regulation: the mechanism by which the perceptual system adjusts the range of stimuli it can process given current demands. In machine vision, aperture and exposure control play the iris role in camera-based systems. Both mappings are conceptual and marked as internal framework language.
