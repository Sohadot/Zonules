# Kinetic Depth Effect

> Depth recovered from changing two-dimensional motion.

## Definition

The kinetic depth effect is the perception of three-dimensional structure from the two-dimensional motion of a rotating object's projection. [CLM-1020]

## Scientific Grounding

A shadow of a bent wire figure looks flat when still but appears solid once it rotates. [CLM-1021] The effect shows that motion alone can specify depth. [CLM-1022]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

The kinetic depth effect is an interpretation-layer recovery: depth read out of changing two-dimensional motion. [CLM-1023]
