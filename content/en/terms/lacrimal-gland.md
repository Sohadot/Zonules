# Lacrimal Gland

> The gland that keeps the eye's front surface optically smooth.

## Definition

The lacrimal gland produces the watery component of the tear film that lubricates and protects the ocular surface. [CLM-848]

## Scientific Grounding

Its secretions are spread across the cornea by blinking and drain through the puncta into the nasolacrimal duct. [CLM-849] The National Eye Institute notes that tears keep the surface of the eye smooth and clear, which is necessary for good vision. [CLM-850]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002, SRC-025. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The lacrimal gland is a suspension-layer maintenance element: it sustains the smooth optical surface on which a sharp image depends. [CLM-851]
