# Lateral Geniculate Nucleus

> Not a passive relay but an active gate, where attention helps decide what the cortex receives.

## Definition

The lateral geniculate nucleus is a layered thalamic relay that receives visual input from the optic tract and projects to the primary visual cortex, maintaining retinotopic organisation and segregating signals into magnocellular and parvocellular streams. [CLM-143]

## Scientific Grounding

The LGN receives substantially more input from cortical feedback than from the retina, making it an active gating station that modulates signals based on attentional state and arousal. [CLM-144]

The National Eye Institute describes vision as arising when signals travel from the retina along the visual pathway to the brain, where they are processed into the images we see. [CLM-840]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005, SRC-025. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The lateral geniculate nucleus is a suspension element of the visual pathway (FIO-01): it maintains the active signal channel between the retina and the visual cortex. [CLM-145]
