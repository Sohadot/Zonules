# Light Adaptation

> Light adaptation is an accommodation mechanism

## Definition

Light adaptation is the process by which the visual system reduces its sensitivity and adjusts its operating range in response to increased illumination. [CLM-364]

## Scientific Grounding

Multiple mechanisms contribute, including pupillary constriction, photoreceptor pigment bleaching, and neural gain control. [CLM-365] Light adaptation allows the visual system to operate across a range of light intensities exceeding a factor of a billion. [CLM-366]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Light adaptation is an accommodation mechanism (FIO-02): it recalibrates the system's input range across luminance environments without changing optical geometry. [CLM-367]
