# Lightness Constancy

> Coal stays black in sunlight; paper stays white indoors.

## Definition

Lightness constancy is the perception of a surface's stable reflectance despite large changes in the amount of light it reflects. [CLM-672]

## Scientific Grounding

A lump of coal in sunlight reflects more light than white paper indoors, yet the coal still looks black. [CLM-673] The visual system estimates reflectance by comparing a surface against its surroundings rather than reading absolute luminance. [CLM-674]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Lightness constancy is an accommodation-layer achievement — the percept is anchored to the object, not to the fluctuating illumination. [CLM-675]
