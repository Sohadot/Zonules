# Limbus

> The border where the clear window meets the white wall.

## Definition

The limbus is the transitional border zone where the transparent cornea meets the opaque sclera and conjunctiva. [CLM-600]

## Scientific Grounding

It houses the limbal stem cells that continuously renew the corneal epithelium. [CLM-601] The limbus also contains the drainage structures at the angle through which aqueous humor leaves the eye. [CLM-602]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The limbus is a structural boundary that maintains the cornea's renewal and the eye's fluid balance — a suspension-layer maintenance zone. [CLM-603]
