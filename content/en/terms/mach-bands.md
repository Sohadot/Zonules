# Mach Bands

> Edge-enhancing machinery invents contrast.

## Definition

Mach bands are illusory bright and dark bands seen at the edges of a luminance gradient. [CLM-992]

## Scientific Grounding

They appear where the gradient changes slope, exaggerating the transition. [CLM-993] The effect is attributed to lateral inhibition in early visual processing. [CLM-994]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Mach bands are a separation-layer artifact: edge-enhancing machinery invents contrast the signal does not contain. [CLM-995]

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Mach Bands is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the [focus-integrity ontology](/focus-integrity-ontology/) is fixed as an FIO-03 — Separation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
