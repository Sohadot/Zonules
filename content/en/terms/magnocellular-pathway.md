# Magnocellular Pathway

> The fast stream tuned to where and when.

## Definition

The magnocellular pathway is a visual stream, originating in parasol ganglion cells, specialized for motion and coarse spatial detail. [CLM-892]

## Scientific Grounding

It conducts signals rapidly and is highly sensitive to low contrast but largely color-blind. [CLM-893] It occupies the two ventral magnocellular layers of the lateral geniculate nucleus. [CLM-894]

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The magnocellular pathway is a separation-layer channel tuned to where and when, not what. [CLM-895]
