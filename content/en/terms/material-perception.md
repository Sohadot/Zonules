# Material Perception

> Substance inferred from how a surface scatters light.

## Definition

Material perception is the visual estimation of what a surface is made of, such as metal, cloth, or water. [CLM-1028]

## Scientific Grounding

Cues including gloss, texture, and the pattern of highlights inform material judgments. [CLM-1029] Observers can often identify materials rapidly from a single image. [CLM-1030]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Material perception is an interpretation-layer reading: it infers substance from the way a surface scatters light. [CLM-1031]
