# Mental Imagery

> Mental imagery is an interpretation of the absence of signal

## Definition

Mental imagery is the experience of visual representations in the absence of corresponding external stimulation. [CLM-448]

## Scientific Grounding

Mental imagery activates overlapping cortical regions with direct visual perception, including early visual areas, though with lower fidelity and resolution. [CLM-449] The ability to generate and manipulate internal visual representations varies substantially across individuals, from vivid imagery to near-absence (aphantasia). [CLM-450]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Mental imagery is an interpretation of the absence of signal (FIO-05): the visual system constructs a representation from internal knowledge rather than incoming sensory data. [CLM-451]
