# Metacognition

> Metacognition is an interpretation of one's own interpretations

## Definition

Metacognition is the capacity to monitor and evaluate one's own cognitive processes, including assessing the confidence and accuracy of one's own perceptual judgments. [CLM-468]

## Scientific Grounding

Perceptual metacognition is separable from perceptual sensitivity: observers can be accurate in their judgments but poorly calibrated in their confidence, or vice versa. [CLM-469] Poor metacognitive accuracy has been linked to deficits in prefrontal function, demonstrating a dissociation between first-order perception and its monitoring. [CLM-470]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Metacognition is an interpretation of one's own interpretations (FIO-05): the confidence signal that guides behavior is itself a constructed output that can fail independently of the primary perceptual judgment. [CLM-471]
