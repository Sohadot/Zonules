# Midget Ganglion Cells

> The retina's channel for fine detail and color.

## Definition

Midget ganglion cells are small retinal ganglion cells that dominate the fovea and carry fine spatial and color detail. [CLM-884]

## Scientific Grounding

Each foveal midget cell can receive input from a single cone, giving very high spatial resolution. [CLM-885] They feed the parvocellular pathway to the lateral geniculate nucleus. [CLM-886]

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Midget cells are a separation-layer channel for detail: they resolve the finest spatial and chromatic signal the retina sends on. [CLM-887]
