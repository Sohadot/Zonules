# Motion Aftereffect

> Stare at a waterfall, then watch the rocks drift up.

## Definition

The motion aftereffect is the illusion of motion in the opposite direction seen after prolonged viewing of steady motion. [CLM-732]

## Scientific Grounding

Adapting to downward motion, for example, makes a stationary scene appear to drift upward. [CLM-733] The effect reflects adaptation in direction-selective motion detectors that become temporarily imbalanced. [CLM-734]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

The motion aftereffect is an accommodation-layer cost — recalibration to one condition distorts the reading of the next. [CLM-735]
