# Motion Parallax

> Move your head and the near rushes past the far.

## Definition

Motion parallax is the depth cue that arises when the observer moves and nearer objects sweep across the retina faster than farther ones. [CLM-684]

## Scientific Grounding

The relative image speed of objects provides information about their relative distance. [CLM-685] It is a monocular cue, available to a single moving eye without binocular input. [CLM-686]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Motion parallax is interpretation-layer evidence — depth read out of the structure of changing motion. [CLM-687]
