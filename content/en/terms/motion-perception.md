# Motion Perception

> Movement is inferred, not directly sampled.

## Definition

Motion perception is the process by which the visual system detects and interprets movement in the visual field. [CLM-276]

## Scientific Grounding

Specialized mechanisms respond selectively to the direction and speed of moving stimuli. [CLM-277] Motion can be perceived even from static or intermittent stimuli under certain conditions. [CLM-278]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Motion perception is an interpretation process (FIO-05): movement is inferred from sampled signals rather than read off directly. [CLM-279]
