# Müller Cells

> The glial scaffold that holds the retina together.

## Definition

Müller cells are the principal glial cells of the retina, spanning its full thickness. [CLM-880]

## Scientific Grounding

They provide structural support and maintain the chemical environment that retinal neurons need to function. [CLM-881] Müller cells also help recycle neurotransmitters and guide light toward the photoreceptors. [CLM-882]

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-011. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Müller cells are a suspension-layer scaffold: they hold the retina's architecture and chemistry stable so the image can be transduced. [CLM-883]
