# Multisensory Integration

> Multisensory integration is an interpretation

## Definition

Multisensory integration is the process by which the brain combines signals from different sensory modalities into a unified percept. [CLM-452]

## Scientific Grounding

Integration follows the principle of maximum likelihood estimation: signals are combined in proportion to their reliability, with more reliable signals given more weight. [CLM-453] Conflicts between modalities are resolved in favor of whichever modality has the highest reliability for that task. [CLM-454]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Multisensory integration is an interpretation (FIO-05): the unified percept of an event is a weighted construction from multiple incomplete signals, not a read-out from any single sensory stream. [CLM-455]
