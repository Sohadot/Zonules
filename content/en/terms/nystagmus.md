# Nystagmus

> Nystagmus is an accommodation failure

## Definition

Nystagmus is an involuntary, rhythmic oscillation of the eyes that impairs fixation stability and is the most common oculomotor disorder. [CLM-432]

## Scientific Grounding

It may be congenital or acquired; acquired nystagmus commonly results from lesions in the brainstem, cerebellum, or vestibular pathways. [CLM-433] The slow drift is the failure of gaze stabilization; the fast corrective saccade is the compensatory reflex. [CLM-434]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Nystagmus is an accommodation failure (FIO-02): the oculomotor system fails to maintain stable gaze, and the fixation point drifts continuously rather than holding. [CLM-435]
