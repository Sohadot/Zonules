# Ocular Dominance Columns

> Where the two eyes' signals come into register.

## Definition

Ocular dominance columns are alternating bands of visual cortex that respond preferentially to input from one eye or the other. [CLM-916]

## Scientific Grounding

Hubel and Wiesel mapped these columns and showed they reorganize if normal binocular input is disrupted during development. [CLM-917] They provide the cortical substrate where the two eyes' signals are brought together. [CLM-918]

*Sources: SRC-010, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Ocular dominance columns are a suspension-layer structure: they hold the two monocular signals in the alignment binocular vision requires. [CLM-919]
