# Oculomotor System

> Focus must be aimed before it can be held; this system sets where high-acuity vision can point.

## Definition

The oculomotor system controls eye movement through six extraocular muscles per eye, producing saccades, smooth pursuit, vergence, and the vestibulo-ocular reflex to direct and stabilise gaze. [CLM-150]

## Scientific Grounding

Vergence eye movements work together with lens accommodation and the pupillary light reflex as the accommodative near triad. [CLM-151] Saccadic suppression reduces visual sensitivity during saccades; the perceived stability of the visual world is a perceptual construction from fixation-period snapshots. [CLM-152]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The oculomotor system defines the accommodation range of gaze (FIO-02): it sets the envelope of positions across which high-acuity fixation can be directed. [CLM-153]
