# Ophthalmic AI

> Machine interpretation applied to the eye, where an interpretation failure carries direct clinical consequence.

## Definition

Ophthalmic AI systems have demonstrated diagnostic performance comparable to specialist clinicians on specific grading tasks such as diabetic retinopathy screening from fundus photographs. [CLM-212]

## Scientific Grounding

Ophthalmic AI performance is sensitive to acquisition device, image quality, patient demographics, and disease prevalence, with performance varying substantially across deployment contexts. [CLM-213] Regulatory clearance for ophthalmic AI requires demonstration of safety and effectiveness across diverse populations and acquisition conditions beyond benchmark performance. [CLM-214]

*Sources: SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

## Machine-Vision Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, ophthalmic AI is classified under FIO-05 Interpretation: the system's task is to interpret retinal images, and interpretation failures carry direct clinical consequence. [CLM-215]
