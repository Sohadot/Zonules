# Optic Chiasm

> Where the two eyes' signals are re-routed so each hemisphere receives one half of the visual world.

## Definition

The optic chiasm is the X-shaped junction at the base of the brain where nasal retinal fibres from each optic nerve cross to the contralateral hemisphere; temporal fibres remain ipsilateral. [CLM-137]

## Scientific Grounding

Compression of the optic chiasm produces bitemporal hemianopia, loss of both temporal visual fields, because the crossing nasal fibres are selectively damaged. [CLM-138]

The National Eye Institute describes visual signals as travelling from the eyes toward the brain along the optic nerves, which partially cross so each hemisphere receives input from both eyes. [CLM-838]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002, SRC-025. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The optic chiasm is a suspension element of the visual pathway (FIO-01): it maintains the structural routing necessary for binocular signal integration. [CLM-139]
