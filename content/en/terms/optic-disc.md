# Optic Disc

> The gateway where the retina's signal leaves the eye.

## Definition

The optic disc is the region of the retina where the axons of the retinal ganglion cells converge and leave the eye to form the optic nerve. [CLM-624]

## Scientific Grounding

Because it contains no photoreceptors, the optic disc corresponds to the physiological blind spot. [CLM-625] The blood vessels of the inner retina enter and exit the eye through the optic disc. [CLM-626]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The optic disc is a suspension-layer junction — the structural gateway through which the visual signal is gathered and routed out of the eye. [CLM-627]
