# Optic Radiations

> The wiring that holds the visual map intact on its way to cortex.

## Definition

The optic radiations are the white-matter fiber tracts carrying visual signals from the lateral geniculate nucleus to the primary visual cortex. [CLM-244]

## Scientific Grounding

Fibers serving different parts of the visual field follow distinct trajectories, including the temporal detour known as Meyer's loop. [CLM-245] Damage to the optic radiations produces characteristic visual field deficits that localize the lesion. [CLM-246]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The optic radiations are a suspension pathway (FIO-01): structure that holds the signal's spatial organization en route to cortex. [CLM-247]
