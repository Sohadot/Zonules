# Optic Tract

> A single non-redundant conduit carrying half the visual world from the chiasm toward the cortex.

## Definition

Each optic tract carries post-chiasmatic visual fibres from the ipsilateral temporal retina and the contralateral nasal retina, representing the contralateral visual field, from the optic chiasm to the lateral geniculate nucleus. [CLM-140]

## Scientific Grounding

The retinotopic organisation of the visual field is preserved through the optic tract: neighbouring fibres carry signals from neighbouring points in the visual field. [CLM-141]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The optic tract is a suspension element of the visual pathway (FIO-01): a non-redundant structural conduit whose failure collapses signal delivery to the cortex. [CLM-142]
