# Optical Aberration

> Every real optical system adds its own noise.

## Definition

Optical aberrations are imperfections in an optical system that prevent light from converging to a single ideal point. [CLM-868]

## Scientific Grounding

The eye exhibits both lower-order aberrations, such as defocus, and higher-order aberrations that degrade fine image quality. [CLM-869] Aberrations reduce retinal image contrast even when the gross focus is correct. [CLM-870]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Optical aberration is a separation-layer limit: it is the residual noise an imperfect optical system adds to every signal it forms. [CLM-871]
