# Optokinetic Reflex

> The optokinetic reflex is a gaze suspension mechanism

## Definition

The optokinetic reflex is a reflexive eye movement that stabilizes the visual image during sustained whole-field motion by rotating the eyes in the direction of motion. [CLM-492]

## Scientific Grounding

The reflex is driven by the direction of global visual motion and is mediated by subcortical pathways distinct from those driving voluntary smooth pursuit. [CLM-493] Like the vestibulo-ocular reflex, it operates with a slow phase in the direction of motion and a fast corrective saccade in the opposite direction. [CLM-494]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The optokinetic reflex is a gaze suspension mechanism (FIO-02): it holds the image stable on the retina during environmental motion, preventing global retinal slip from destroying acuity. [CLM-495]
