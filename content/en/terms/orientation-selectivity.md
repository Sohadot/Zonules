# Orientation Selectivity

> The cortex's first reading of oriented edges.

## Definition

Orientation selectivity is the property of many visual cortical neurons that respond most strongly to edges or bars at a particular orientation. [CLM-912]

## Scientific Grounding

Hubel and Wiesel found that such neurons are tuned to the angle of a contour within their receptive field. [CLM-913] Orientation-tuned neurons are organized into systematic columns across the cortex. [CLM-914]

*Sources: SRC-010, SRC-009. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Orientation selectivity is a separation-layer primitive: it extracts the oriented edges from which form is built. [CLM-915]
