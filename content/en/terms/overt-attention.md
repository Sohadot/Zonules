# Overt Attention

> Focus held by aiming the eye's sharpest region.

## Definition

Overt attention is the selection of a location or object accompanied by movement of the eyes to fixate it. [CLM-1040]

## Scientific Grounding

Most everyday viewing uses overt shifts, moving the high-resolution fovea to points of interest. [CLM-1041] Eye-movement records are widely used to infer where overt attention is directed. [CLM-1042]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Overt attention is a suspension-layer act: it holds focus by aiming the eye's sharpest region at the target. [CLM-1043]
