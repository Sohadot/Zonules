# Parasol Ganglion Cells

> The retina's channel for motion and contrast.

## Definition

Parasol ganglion cells are large retinal ganglion cells with wide dendritic fields that pool input from many photoreceptors. [CLM-888]

## Scientific Grounding

They respond quickly and transiently and are sensitive to motion and low-contrast luminance changes. [CLM-889] They feed the magnocellular pathway to the lateral geniculate nucleus. [CLM-890]

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Parasol cells are a separation-layer channel for change: they extract motion and contrast from the retinal signal. [CLM-891]
