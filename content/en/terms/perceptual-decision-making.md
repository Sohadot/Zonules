# Perceptual Decision-Making

> The point at which a reading of the signal is declared.

## Definition

Perceptual decision-making is the process of committing to an interpretation of sensory evidence. [CLM-976]

## Scientific Grounding

It is often modeled as the accumulation of evidence until a decision threshold is reached. [CLM-977] The threshold trades speed against accuracy: lower thresholds yield faster but more error-prone decisions. [CLM-978]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role
