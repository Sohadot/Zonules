# Perceptual Error

> Perceptual error is the visible output of interpretation failure: a clear signal given the wrong meaning.

## Definition

Perceptual errors arise from systematic biases in sensory processing, context effects, prior expectations, and limits of neural computation, and are distinct from sensory noise. [CLM-180]

## Scientific Grounding

Optical illusions are canonical demonstrations of perceptual error, where the visual system's heuristics for interpreting depth, size, and brightness produce demonstrably incorrect interpretations. [CLM-181] Perceptual errors are not random failures but often the predictable output of adaptively useful heuristics operating outside their domain of validity. [CLM-182]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, perceptual error is the observable output of FIO-05 Interpretation failure: the point where a well-transmitted signal is assigned an incorrect meaning. [CLM-183]

## Mechanism

Perceptual errors arise when the heuristics that normally recover the world from ambiguous input are applied to input that violates their assumptions, yielding a confident but wrong percept. [CLM-1307]

## Why It Matters

Errors are not noise to be dismissed; they are the clearest evidence of how perception works, revealing the inferences the system makes by showing where they break. [CLM-1308] Perceptual error is the canonical interpretation failure: focus is held and the signal is clean, yet the meaning is wrong. [CLM-1309]

## Human–Machine Bridge

Machine perception shows the same signature in adversarial examples and shortcut learning: a sharp input read with confident, incorrect meaning. [CLM-1310] Sharing the FIO-05 class lets one standard ask of a person and a model whether clean focus produced the correct reading. [CLM-1311]

## Common Misunderstanding

A perceptual error is often taken to mean poor eyesight; in fact illusions fool observers with perfect optics, because the error is in interpretation, not in the signal. [CLM-1312]
