# Perceptual Learning

> Practice retunes the senses, not just the response.

## Definition

Perceptual learning is the lasting improvement in perceptual ability that follows practice with a sensory task. [CLM-720]

## Scientific Grounding

Training can sharpen discriminations such as fine orientation or texture differences, sometimes specific to the trained location or feature. [CLM-721] The improvements reflect changes in how sensory information is weighted and read out, not changes in the stimulus. [CLM-722]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-009. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Perceptual learning is an accommodation-layer change — the system retunes its range to the demands it has met. [CLM-723]
