# Perceptual Load

> Attention has an aperture, and how much the scene demands decides how wide it can open.

## Definition

Perceptual load theory proposes that high-load tasks exhaust limited attentional capacity, suppressing processing of task-irrelevant stimuli automatically. [CLM-168]

## Scientific Grounding

Under low perceptual load, spare cognitive capacity is allocated to distractors, increasing susceptibility to irrelevant stimulation. [CLM-169] Perceptual load interacts with selective attention, with high load producing more focused, effortless exclusion of competing visual information than low load conditions. [CLM-170]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, perceptual load maps to FIO-02 Accommodation: it defines the effective aperture of attentional focus available to any given visual scene. [CLM-171]
