# Photopic Vision

> Photopic vision is the high-signal operating mode of the visual system

## Definition

Photopic vision is the visual function of the eye under well-lit conditions, mediated by cone photoreceptors. [CLM-424]

## Scientific Grounding

Cones are concentrated in and around the fovea; the three cone types (S, M, L) underlie trichromatic color vision. [CLM-425] Photopic vision has high spatial acuity and color discrimination but lower sensitivity than scotopic vision. [CLM-426]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Photopic vision is the high-signal operating mode of the visual system (FIO-03): it maximizes signal resolution and color discrimination at the cost of absolute sensitivity. [CLM-427]
