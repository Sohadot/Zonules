# Photoreceptors

> Below their threshold there is no signal. Above it, what enters the eye either resolves or does not, and the photoreceptor is where that decision is made.

## Definition

**Photoreceptors** are the specialized cells of the retina that transduce light into neural signals. There are two types: **rods**, which are sensitive to low-light conditions and to movement across the peripheral visual field, and **cones**, which are concentrated at the fovea and are responsible for color vision and high-acuity detail. [CLM-052]

## Scientific Grounding

The **fovea** — the small central pit of the retina — contains the highest concentration of cone photoreceptors and is the anatomical seat of sharp central vision. [CLM-053] When the eye fixates on an object, it positions the image of that object on the fovea. The zone of sharp focus is therefore not a property of the whole retina; it is a local consequence of receptor distribution.

Photoreceptor density and distribution across the retina determine the spatial resolution and luminance sensitivity available to the visual system across the visual field. [CLM-054] The periphery, rich in rods, is sensitive to motion and low light but cannot resolve fine detail. This is not a failure; it is a structural trade-off built into the signal-noise architecture of the eye.

*Sources: ocular basic science and sensation and perception references (SRC-001, SRC-005). See the source registry for classification.*

## The Signal-Noise Boundary

Photoreceptors are the signal-noise boundary of anatomy. Below their activation threshold, there is no visual signal — the eye produces no response, regardless of the quality of the optical system upstream. Above that threshold, what the photoreceptors receive is what the visual system has to work with. [CLM-055]

This is a structural fact about vision that maps directly to the focus-integrity thesis: the signal that can be interpreted depends on the transducers that convert it. An intact suspension system, a clear cornea, and a well-focused lens all serve to deliver a high-quality signal to this boundary.

## Structural Position

Photoreceptors occupy a specific position in the visual chain: they are the end of the optical system and the beginning of the neural system. Everything that precedes them — the cornea's refractive power, the lens's accommodation, the zonules' tension — is preparation for this moment. Everything that follows — the retinal processing, the optic nerve, the visual cortex — depends on it.

## Relationship to Perception (Layer 02)

In perception, the analogue of photoreceptor sensitivity is sensory threshold: the point below which stimuli produce no perceptual response. Attention and expectation can shift this threshold, but they cannot recover signal that never entered. This is a conceptual mapping, marked as internal framework language.
