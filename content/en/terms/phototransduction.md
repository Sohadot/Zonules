# Phototransduction

> How light is turned into the language of nerves.

## Definition

Phototransduction is the process by which a photoreceptor converts absorbed light into an electrical signal. [CLM-652]

## Scientific Grounding

In darkness photoreceptors are depolarized; light closes ion channels and hyperpolarizes the cell, reducing its release of neurotransmitter. [CLM-653] The cascade amplifies a single absorbed photon into a measurable change in the cell's membrane current. [CLM-654]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-012, SRC-009. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Phototransduction is the entry gate of the separation layer — the conversion that decides what counts as signal. [CLM-655]
