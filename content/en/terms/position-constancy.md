# Position Constancy

> The scene held still against the motion we add.

## Definition

Position constancy is the perception of a stable world despite the constant motion of the retinal image caused by eye movements. [CLM-1060]

## Scientific Grounding

The visual system uses information about its own eye movements to discount the resulting image motion. [CLM-1061] When this compensation fails, the world appears to move with the eyes. [CLM-1062]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Position constancy is an accommodation-layer correction: the scene is held still against the motion the observer adds. [CLM-1063]

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Position Constancy is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the [focus-integrity ontology](/focus-integrity-ontology/) is fixed as an FIO-02 — Accommodation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
