# Prior Expectation

> The weight the past places on the present reading.

## Definition

A prior expectation is prior knowledge or bias that shapes how an ambiguous stimulus is perceived. [CLM-964]

## Scientific Grounding

Strong priors can make perception faster but can also cause confident misperception when they are wrong. [CLM-965] Priors are learned from the statistical regularities of past experience. [CLM-966]

*Sources: SRC-006, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Prior expectation is an interpretation-layer weight: it tilts the reading of the signal toward what was likely before. [CLM-967]
