# Pupil

> The aperture that trades light for sharpness.

## Definition

The pupil is the central aperture of the iris through which light enters the eye. [CLM-656]

## Scientific Grounding

Its diameter is set by the opposing muscles of the iris and controls how much light reaches the retina. [CLM-657] A smaller pupil increases depth of field and reduces optical aberrations, while a larger pupil admits more light in dim conditions. [CLM-658]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The pupil is a separation-layer aperture: it governs the trade-off between light gathered and image sharpness. [CLM-659]
