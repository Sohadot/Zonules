# Refraction

> The bending of light into a focal plane.

## Definition

Refraction is the bending of light as it passes between media of different optical density, which the eye uses to focus images on the retina. [CLM-864]

## Scientific Grounding

Most of the eye's refractive power comes from the cornea, with the lens providing adjustable fine focus. [CLM-865] The total refractive power must match the eye's length for distant images to fall sharply on the retina. [CLM-866]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-001. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Refraction is the accommodation layer's foundation: it is the act of bending light into a focal plane the system can hold. [CLM-867]
