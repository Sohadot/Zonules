# Retinal Ganglion Cells

> The retina's final encoding step decides what the brain ever receives.

## Definition

Retinal ganglion cells are the output neurons of the retina whose axons form the optic nerve and carry visual signals to the brain. [CLM-224]

## Scientific Grounding

They receive processed input from bipolar and amacrine cells and encode features such as contrast and motion before transmission. [CLM-225] Distinct ganglion cell populations, including magnocellular and parvocellular pathways, convey different aspects of the visual signal. [CLM-226]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Retinal ganglion cells are the interpretation gateway of the eye (FIO-05): the final retinal encoding stage that determines what reaches the brain. [CLM-227]
