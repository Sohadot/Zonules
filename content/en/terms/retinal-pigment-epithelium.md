# Retinal Pigment Epithelium

> Invisible maintenance that keeps the photoreceptors working day after day.

## Definition

The retinal pigment epithelium (RPE) is a single layer of pigmented cells between the photoreceptors and the choroid that supports photoreceptor function. [CLM-248]

## Scientific Grounding

The RPE recycles visual pigment, phagocytoses shed photoreceptor outer segments, and absorbs stray light. [CLM-249] RPE dysfunction is implicated in retinal degenerations such as age-related macular degeneration. [CLM-250]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The RPE is a maintenance-suspension layer (FIO-01): hidden support that keeps the photoreceptors operating. [CLM-251]
