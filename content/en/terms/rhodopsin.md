# Rhodopsin

> The molecule where a photon first becomes a signal.

## Definition

Rhodopsin is the light-sensitive pigment in rod photoreceptors, made of the protein opsin bound to a retinal chromophore. [CLM-648]

## Scientific Grounding

Absorbing a photon changes the shape of the retinal molecule, triggering the cascade that signals light. [CLM-649] The slow regeneration of rhodopsin after bleaching underlies the time course of dark adaptation. [CLM-650]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Rhodopsin is the molecular threshold of the separation layer — the first point at which a photon becomes signal. [CLM-651]
