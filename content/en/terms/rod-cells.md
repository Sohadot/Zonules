# Rod Cells

> The photoreceptors that see in near-darkness.

## Definition

Rod cells are the photoreceptors responsible for vision in dim light, far more numerous than cones in the human retina. [CLM-640]

## Scientific Grounding

A single rod can respond to an individual photon, giving the rod system extreme sensitivity. [CLM-641] Rods are absent from the foveola and reach their highest density in a ring in the peripheral retina. [CLM-642]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Rods are a separation-layer instrument tuned for low signal: they pull a usable percept out of near-darkness. [CLM-643]
