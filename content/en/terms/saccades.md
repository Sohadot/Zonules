# Saccades

> The eye re-aims itself several times a second to keep focus where it matters.

## Definition

Saccades are rapid ballistic eye movements that shift the fovea from one point of interest to another. [CLM-232]

## Scientific Grounding

During a saccade visual sensitivity is briefly reduced, a phenomenon called saccadic suppression. [CLM-233] Saccades reposition the high-acuity fovea because detailed vision is limited to a small central region of the retina. [CLM-234]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Saccades are an accommodation mechanism of gaze (FIO-02): they repeatedly re-aim the eye to hold focus on changing targets. [CLM-235]
