# Scene Recognition

> Scene recognition is an interpretation

## Definition

Scene recognition is the process by which the visual system assigns a global meaning to a visual environment from its spatial layout and statistical regularities. [CLM-396]

## Scientific Grounding

Scene category can be extracted in under 100 milliseconds, before individual objects are identified, from the spatial frequency envelope and global composition of the image. [CLM-397] The gist of a scene is represented before local detail is resolved, indicating that interpretation is not purely bottom-up. [CLM-398]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Scene recognition is an interpretation (FIO-05): the meaning of a scene is assigned by the visual system to the global signal before the local signal is fully analyzed. [CLM-399]
