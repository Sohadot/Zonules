# Sclera

> The geometric shell that holds every optical component in its correct spatial relationship.

## Definition

The sclera is the tough, fibrous outer coat of the eye forming approximately five-sixths of the globe's outer surface, maintaining the eye's spherical shape and providing extraocular muscle insertion points. [CLM-154]

## Scientific Grounding

At the optic nerve head the sclera forms the lamina cribrosa through which retinal ganglion cell axons exit; elevated intraocular pressure damages this structure in glaucoma. [CLM-155]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The sclera is a suspension element of the eye's optical system (FIO-01): it maintains the geometric shell within which the optical components are held in their correct spatial relationships. [CLM-156]
