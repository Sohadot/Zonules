# Scotoma

> A scotoma is a provenance gap

## Definition

A scotoma is a region of absent or diminished vision within the visual field surrounded by areas of normal or relatively normal vision. [CLM-436]

## Scientific Grounding

Scotomas arise from damage to retinal tissue, the optic nerve, or higher visual pathways; their shape and location map to the anatomical site of the lesion. [CLM-437] The physiological scotoma at the optic disc is not perceived because the visual system fills it in using surrounding information — the same process as the blind spot. [CLM-438]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

A scotoma is a provenance gap (FIO-04): the visual system receives no signal from a region of the world yet may not register its own absence, making the gap invisible as a gap. [CLM-439]
