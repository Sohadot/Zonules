# Scotopic Vision

> Scotopic vision operates at the lower signal boundary of the visual system

## Definition

Scotopic vision is the visual function of the eye under dim light conditions, mediated by rod photoreceptors. [CLM-428]

## Scientific Grounding

Rods are distributed throughout the peripheral retina, are absent from the fovea, and are roughly 1000 times more sensitive to light than cones. [CLM-429] Scotopic vision lacks color discrimination and has lower spatial resolution than photopic vision. [CLM-430]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Scotopic vision operates at the lower signal boundary of the visual system (FIO-03): it extends dynamic range at the cost of signal specificity, trading resolution for detection. [CLM-431]
