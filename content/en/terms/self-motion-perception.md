# Self-Motion Perception

> The world is stable; the self is moving.

## Definition

Self-motion perception is the sense of one's own movement through the environment, drawn from visual and vestibular cues. [CLM-1012]

## Scientific Grounding

The expanding pattern of optic flow across the retina is a powerful visual cue to self-motion. [CLM-1013] Conflicts between visual and vestibular signals can produce illusions of movement. [CLM-1014]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Self-motion perception is an interpretation-layer reading: it tells the observer that the world is stable and the self is moving. [CLM-1015]
