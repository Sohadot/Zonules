# Simple Cells

> The cortex's oriented edge filters.

## Definition

Simple cells are primary visual cortex neurons with receptive fields divided into distinct excitatory and inhibitory regions. [CLM-920]

## Scientific Grounding

They respond best to an edge or bar of a specific orientation and position. [CLM-921] Their behavior can be approximated by a linear filter followed by a threshold. [CLM-922]

*Sources: SRC-010, SRC-009. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Simple cells are a separation-layer filter: they convert the retinal image into a map of oriented edges. [CLM-923]
