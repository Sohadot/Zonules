# Simultaneous Contrast

> A region is read against its surround, not alone.

## Definition

Simultaneous contrast is the shift in a region's apparent brightness or color caused by its surroundings. [CLM-988]

## Scientific Grounding

An identical gray patch looks lighter on a dark background and darker on a light one. [CLM-989] The effect reflects lateral interactions that emphasize the differences between adjacent regions. [CLM-990]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Simultaneous contrast is a separation-layer bias: the system reads a region against its surround, not in absolute terms. [CLM-991]
