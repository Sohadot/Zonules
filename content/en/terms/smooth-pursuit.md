# Smooth Pursuit

> Holding a moving target still on the fovea by moving with it.

## Definition

Smooth pursuit is a voluntary eye movement that tracks a moving target to keep its image stable on the fovea. [CLM-236]

## Scientific Grounding

Unlike saccades, smooth pursuit continuously matches eye velocity to target velocity. [CLM-237] Smooth pursuit generally requires a moving stimulus and cannot be sustained over a stationary scene. [CLM-238]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Smooth pursuit is a continuous accommodation of gaze (FIO-02): focus held on a target across its motion. [CLM-239]
