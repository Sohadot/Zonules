# Spatial Attention

> Spatial attention is a suspension mechanism

## Definition

Spatial attention is the selective enhancement of processing for stimuli at a particular location in the visual field, operating like a spotlight that brightens one region of the scene. [CLM-532]

## Scientific Grounding

Attending to a location increases the apparent contrast of stimuli there, speeds detection responses, and reduces the contrast threshold for target detection. [CLM-533] Spatial attention can be covertly directed to locations other than the point of fixation, demonstrating that the locus of attention and the locus of gaze are distinct. [CLM-534]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Spatial attention is a suspension mechanism (FIO-01): it holds a selected region of the visual scene in enhanced processing, maintaining the focus of analysis independent of the direction of gaze. [CLM-535]
