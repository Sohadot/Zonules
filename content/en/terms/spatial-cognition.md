# Spatial Cognition

> Spatial cognition is an interpretation

## Definition

Spatial cognition is the set of mental processes by which the brain represents, navigates, and reasons about spatial relationships and locations in the environment. [CLM-456]

## Scientific Grounding

Hippocampal place cells and entorhinal grid cells form a neural substrate for allocentric spatial representation — a map of the environment independent of the observer's current position. [CLM-457] Spatial memory is constructed incrementally through experience and can be severely disrupted by hippocampal damage while leaving other forms of memory intact. [CLM-458]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Spatial cognition is an interpretation (FIO-05): the brain's map of space is a constructed model maintained by inference rather than a direct read-out of environmental structure. [CLM-459]
