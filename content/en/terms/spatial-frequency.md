# Spatial Frequency

> Spatial frequency is a signal decomposition axis

## Definition

Spatial frequency describes the periodicity of luminance variation across space, measured in cycles per degree of visual angle, and is a fundamental description of the visual signal. [CLM-500]

## Scientific Grounding

The visual system analyzes the image through channels tuned to different spatial frequencies: low frequencies carry coarse shape and layout; high frequencies carry fine detail and edges. [CLM-501] The contrast sensitivity function describes the visual system's sensitivity at each spatial frequency, peaking at intermediate frequencies and falling off at high and low extremes. [CLM-502]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Spatial frequency is a signal decomposition axis (FIO-03): the visual system reads the world as a multi-channel signal in which information at different scales is separated before being integrated. [CLM-503]
