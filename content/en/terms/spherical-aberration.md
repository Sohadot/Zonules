# Spherical Aberration

> The aperture that gathers more light also blurs it.

## Definition

Spherical aberration occurs when light passing through the periphery of a lens focuses at a different point than light through its center. [CLM-876]

## Scientific Grounding

A larger pupil admits more peripheral rays and therefore increases spherical aberration. [CLM-877] The cornea and lens of the eye partly offset each other's spherical aberration. [CLM-878]

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

Spherical aberration is a separation-layer trade-off: the same aperture that gathers more light also degrades its focus. [CLM-879]
