# Synthetic Media

> When appearance no longer proves origin, authenticity must be carried by an independent provenance chain.

## Definition

Synthetic media encompasses outputs of generative systems including GAN-generated images, diffusion model outputs, NeRF renderings, and text-to-image synthesis. [CLM-200]

## Scientific Grounding

The indistinguishability of synthetic media from authentic capture constitutes a provenance integrity failure: origin can no longer be inferred from appearance alone. [CLM-201] The C2PA standard provides cryptographically anchored metadata recording origin, edit history, and tool provenance, enabling authenticity verification independent of pixel analysis. [CLM-202]

*Sources: SRC-007, SRC-008. See the source registry for classification.*

## Machine-Vision Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, synthetic media is classified under FIO-04 Provenance: the structural challenge is that appearance alone cannot establish origin, requiring an independent provenance chain. [CLM-203]
