# Tear Film

> The first optical surface light meets is made of fluid.

## Definition

The tear film is the thin fluid layer coating the ocular surface that maintains a smooth optical surface on the cornea. [CLM-264]

## Scientific Grounding

It has lipid, aqueous, and mucin components that together lubricate, nourish, and protect the surface. [CLM-265] An unstable or deficient tear film degrades the corneal optical surface and visual quality, as in dry eye. [CLM-266]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The tear film is a signal-noise layer (FIO-03): the first optical surface, kept clear so light enters uncorrupted. [CLM-267]
