# Text-to-Image Generation

> Convincing depictions of events that never happened.

## Definition

Text-to-image generation produces images from natural-language descriptions. [CLM-1072]

## Scientific Grounding

Modern systems condition a generative model, often a diffusion model, on a text embedding. [CLM-1073] The resulting photorealistic images of scenes that never occurred sharpen provenance concerns. [CLM-1074]

*Sources: SRC-017, SRC-023. See the source registry for classification.*

## Machine-Vision Role

Text-to-image generation is a provenance-layer challenge: it manufactures convincing depictions of events that never happened. [CLM-1075]
