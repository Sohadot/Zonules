# Top-Down Processing

> Prior knowledge guides interpretation, and can override an accurate signal with what was expected.

## Definition

Top-down processing uses stored knowledge, contextual expectations, and prior experience to guide interpretation of incoming visual signals, often overriding raw sensory evidence. [CLM-172]

## Scientific Grounding

Context effects in visual perception — such as reading ambiguous letters differently depending on surrounding words — demonstrate the power of top-down priors. [CLM-173] Top-down and bottom-up processing interact continuously; neither operates in isolation in natural visual scenes, with each modulating the other across processing hierarchies. [CLM-174]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, top-down processing is the mechanism most directly implicated in FIO-05 Interpretation failures, as prior knowledge can systematically distort reading of an accurate signal. [CLM-175]
