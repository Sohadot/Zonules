# Trabecular Meshwork

> The drain that keeps the eye's internal pressure—and its shape—stable.

## Definition

The trabecular meshwork is the spongy tissue at the iridocorneal angle through which aqueous humor drains from the eye. [CLM-256]

## Scientific Grounding

It regulates aqueous outflow and thereby helps maintain intraocular pressure. [CLM-257] Impaired outflow through the trabecular meshwork is a major mechanism of open-angle glaucoma. [CLM-258]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*
