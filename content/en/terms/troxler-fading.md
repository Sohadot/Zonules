# Troxler Fading

> A signal that never changes becomes no signal.

## Definition

Troxler fading is the disappearance of a steady peripheral stimulus during prolonged fixation. [CLM-1000]

## Scientific Grounding

It occurs because unchanging input on stable retinal locations is adapted away. [CLM-1001] Small eye movements normally refresh the image and prevent such fading. [CLM-1002]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Troxler fading is an accommodation-layer side effect: a signal that never changes is treated as no signal at all. [CLM-1003]

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Troxler Fading is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the [focus-integrity ontology](/focus-integrity-ontology/) is fixed as an FIO-02 — Accommodation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
