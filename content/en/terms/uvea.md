# Uvea

> The pigmented middle coat that supports the optics.

## Definition

The uvea is the pigmented middle layer of the eye, comprising the iris, the ciliary body, and the choroid. [CLM-660]

## Scientific Grounding

It supplies most of the blood to the eye and absorbs stray light to prevent internal scatter. [CLM-661] Its three structures together control the aperture, drive accommodation, and nourish the outer retina. [CLM-662]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The uvea is a suspension-layer support system: it maintains the light environment and blood supply that hold the optical system in working order. [CLM-663]
