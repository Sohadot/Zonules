# Velocity Constancy

> Perceived speed held steady as distance changes.

## Definition

Velocity constancy is the tendency to perceive an object's speed as stable despite changes in the speed of its retinal image with distance. [CLM-1056]

## Scientific Grounding

A car seen far away crosses the retina slowly yet is perceived as moving fast. [CLM-1057] The visual system scales retinal speed by perceived distance, as it does for size. [CLM-1058]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Velocity constancy is an accommodation-layer achievement: perceived speed held stable as viewing distance changes. [CLM-1059]
