# Ventral Stream

> The pathway for what things are.

## Definition

The ventral stream is the cortical visual pathway running from the visual cortex toward the temporal lobe, associated with object recognition and identity. [CLM-904]

## Scientific Grounding

It is often summarized as the what pathway. [CLM-905] It depends substantially on detailed parvocellular input. [CLM-906]

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

## Anatomical Role

The ventral stream is an interpretation-layer route: it reads the scene for what things are. [CLM-907]

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Ventral Stream is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result.
