# Vergence

> Vergence is an accommodation mechanism

## Definition

Vergence refers to the disjunctive movements of the two eyes that maintain alignment of the visual axes on objects at different distances. [CLM-380]

## Scientific Grounding

Convergence and divergence are driven by disparity signals from corresponding retinal points and by accommodative demand. [CLM-381] Vergence and accommodation are neurally coupled: the vergence-accommodation reflex links the focusing effort of the lens to the binocular alignment of the eyes. [CLM-382]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Vergence is an accommodation mechanism (FIO-02): it maintains the geometric alignment required for stereopsis and binocular single vision as viewing distance changes. [CLM-383]
