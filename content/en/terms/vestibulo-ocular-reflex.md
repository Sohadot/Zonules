# Vestibulo-Ocular Reflex

> The vestibulo-ocular reflex is a gaze suspension mechanism

## Definition

The vestibulo-ocular reflex stabilizes gaze during head movement by producing compensatory eye movements in the opposite direction to head rotation. [CLM-496]

## Scientific Grounding

The reflex arc connects the semicircular canals of the inner ear to the extraocular muscles via the brainstem, operating with extremely short latency of approximately 10 milliseconds. [CLM-497] The VOR allows clear vision during locomotion and rapid head movement, and its gain can be adaptively recalibrated through experience. [CLM-498]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The vestibulo-ocular reflex is a gaze suspension mechanism (FIO-02): it counteracts head rotation to keep the retinal image stable — the neurological thread that holds focus in place during body movement. [CLM-499]
