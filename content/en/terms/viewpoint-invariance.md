# Viewpoint Invariance

> Identity held stable across the angles a thing is seen.

## Definition

Viewpoint invariance is the ability to recognize an object across changes in viewing angle. [CLM-1048]

## Scientific Grounding

Recognition is often slower for unusual or unfamiliar viewpoints, suggesting partial rather than complete invariance. [CLM-1049] Theories differ on whether recognition stores multiple views or recovers a viewpoint-independent structure. [CLM-1050]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-015. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Viewpoint invariance is an interpretation-layer constancy: identity held stable across the angles a thing is seen from. [CLM-1051]
