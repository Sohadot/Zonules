# Visual Crowding

> Visual crowding is a signal-noise failure

## Definition

Visual crowding is the impaired recognition of peripheral targets caused by the presence of nearby flanking elements. [CLM-400]

## Scientific Grounding

Crowding is a fundamental limit on peripheral vision: the critical spacing between target and flanker scales with eccentricity (Bouma's law). [CLM-401] Crowding cannot be overcome by attention or practice and reflects a hard architectural constraint on how the visual system processes peripheral signals. [CLM-402]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Visual crowding is a signal-noise failure (FIO-03): flankers degrade the target signal in the peripheral channel in a way that cannot be resolved by higher-level interpretation. [CLM-403]

Visual Crowding is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result.
