# Visual Field

> The visual field is a provenance boundary

## Definition

The visual field is the spatial extent of the environment that can be perceived when fixation is held steady, bounded by the anatomy of the retina and optic pathways. [CLM-376]

## Scientific Grounding

The central visual field is processed by the fovea and has the highest spatial resolution; peripheral regions are progressively less resolved. [CLM-377] Defects in the visual field correspond to lesions at specific points along the visual pathway, making field mapping a tool for localizing pathology. [CLM-378]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

The visual field is a provenance boundary (FIO-04): it defines the scope of what the system can receive, and any region outside it is an unexamined source rather than a confirmed absence. [CLM-379]
