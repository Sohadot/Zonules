# Visual Illusions

> When the system's inferences override the signal.

## Definition

Visual illusions are systematic discrepancies between the perceived and physical properties of a stimulus. [CLM-308]

## Scientific Grounding

They reveal the constructive assumptions the visual system makes about the world. [CLM-309] Many illusions persist even when the viewer knows the true nature of the stimulus. [CLM-310]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Visual illusions are interpretation errors (FIO-05): the system's inferences override the available signal. [CLM-311]

## Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Visual Illusions is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the [focus-integrity ontology](/focus-integrity-ontology/) is fixed as a FIO-05 — Interpretation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
