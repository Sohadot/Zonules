# Visual Masking

> A present signal can be erased by competing input around it.

## Definition

Visual masking is the reduction or elimination of the visibility of one stimulus by another presented in close spatial or temporal proximity. [CLM-284]

## Scientific Grounding

Masking can occur when the mask precedes the target (forward masking) or follows it (backward masking). [CLM-285] Masking is widely used to study the time course of visual processing. [CLM-286]

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

## Perceptual Role

Visual masking is a signal-noise failure (FIO-03): a present signal is suppressed by competing input. [CLM-287]
