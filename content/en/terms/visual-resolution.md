# Visual Resolution

> Visual resolution is a signal discrimination limit

## Definition

Visual resolution is the ability of the eye to distinguish fine detail, measured as the minimum angular separation of two points perceived as distinct. [CLM-508]

## Scientific Grounding

Maximum visual resolution occurs at the fovea, where cone density is highest; resolution falls steeply with eccentricity. [CLM-509] Clinical visual acuity (Snellen chart) measures high-contrast resolution and may understate the impact of conditions that primarily reduce contrast sensitivity. [CLM-510]

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

## Structural Role

Visual resolution is a signal discrimination limit (FIO-03): it defines the spatial grain of the signal channel and determines what spatial information can enter the system at all. [CLM-511]
