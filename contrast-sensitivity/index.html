Contrast Sensitivity

Detail is useless if a figure cannot lift off its background.

Definition

Contrast sensitivity is the visual system's ability to distinguish luminance differences between adjacent regions.

Scientific Grounding

It varies with spatial frequency, a relationship described by the contrast sensitivity function. Contrast sensitivity can be reduced even when fine-detail acuity is preserved.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Contrast sensitivity is a signal-noise capacity (FIO-03): the threshold at which a figure separates from its background.
