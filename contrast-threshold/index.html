Contrast Threshold

The contrast threshold is the signal-noise boundary of the visual system

Definition

The contrast threshold is the minimum difference in luminance between a target and its background that is reliably detectable by the visual system.

Scientific Grounding

Threshold contrast depends on spatial frequency, background luminance, stimulus duration, and eccentricity; it is the fundamental limit of signal detection in vision. Clinical measurement of contrast sensitivity is more sensitive to early retinal and neural pathology than measurement of high-contrast acuity alone.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

The contrast threshold is the signal-noise boundary of the visual system (FIO-03): it defines the minimum signal-to-noise ratio required for a stimulus to be resolved.
