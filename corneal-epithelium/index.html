Corneal Epithelium

The eye's renewing front surface, where the image first forms.

Definition

The corneal epithelium is the outermost cellular layer of the cornea, a stratified non-keratinized surface that forms the eye's first refracting interface with air.

Scientific Grounding

It regenerates rapidly from limbal stem cells, so minor surface abrasions can heal within days. The epithelium together with the overlying tear film creates the smooth optical surface responsible for most of the eye's refractive power.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

As a renewing protective surface, the epithelium is a suspension-layer element: it maintains the boundary conditions under which a sharp image can form.
