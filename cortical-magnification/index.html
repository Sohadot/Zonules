Cortical Magnification

The cortex spends its resolution where signal matters.

Definition

Cortical magnification is the disproportionate amount of visual cortex devoted to the central visual field relative to the periphery.

Scientific Grounding

A tiny foveal region maps onto a large cortical area, mirroring the fovea's dense photoreceptor sampling. This magnification underlies the steep fall of acuity from the center toward the periphery.

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Cortical magnification is a separation-layer allocation: the system spends its resolution where the signal matters most.
