Cue Integration

Several weak signals fused into one confident reading.

Definition

Cue integration is the combination of multiple, separately ambiguous cues into a single more reliable percept.

Scientific Grounding

Cues are typically weighted by their reliability, with more reliable cues dominating the result. Optimal integration can yield a percept more precise than any single cue alone.

*Sources: SRC-006, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Cue integration is an interpretation-layer synthesis: several weak signals are fused into one confident reading.
