Dark Adaptation

Dark adaptation is an accommodation mechanism

Definition

Dark adaptation is the process by which the visual system increases its sensitivity after exposure to bright conditions, driven by photoreceptor recovery.

Scientific Grounding

The process occurs in two phases: an initial rapid cone-mediated phase followed by a slower rod-mediated phase that achieves full sensitivity recovery. Recovery of rod pigment rhodopsin, bleached by bright light, underlies the slow phase and determines its time course.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Dark adaptation is an accommodation mechanism (FIO-02): the optical system dynamically extends its operating range to recover signal in low-luminance environments.
