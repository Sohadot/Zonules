Diffusion Models

Images conjured from noise, with no real referent.

Definition

Diffusion models generate images by learning to reverse a gradual process that turns data into noise.

Scientific Grounding

Starting from noise, the model denoises step by step to produce a sample resembling its training distribution. They produce highly realistic synthetic imagery, which raises provenance and authentication concerns.

*Sources: SRC-017, SRC-023. See the source registry for classification.*

Machine-Vision Role

Diffusion models are a provenance-layer challenge — they manufacture convincing images with no real-world referent.
