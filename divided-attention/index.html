Divided Attention

Two tasks at once, and focus spread too thin for both.

Definition

Divided attention is the attempt to process two or more sources or tasks at the same time.

Scientific Grounding

Performance typically declines as tasks compete for limited capacity, especially when they draw on the same resources. Some highly practiced combinations can be performed together with little cost, while novel or similar tasks interfere strongly.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Divided attention is a suspension-layer stress test — it reveals the limits of how much focus can be held at once.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Divided Attention is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-01 — Suspension concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
