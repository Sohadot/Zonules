Dorsal Stream

The pathway for where things are and how to act.

Definition

The dorsal stream is the cortical visual pathway running from the visual cortex toward the parietal lobe, associated with spatial location and visually guided action.

Scientific Grounding

It is often summarized as the where, or how, pathway. It draws heavily on fast magnocellular input.

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

The dorsal stream is an interpretation-layer route: it reads the scene for where things are and how to act on them.

Layer Classification

  • Layer: L1 — Anatomy
  • Cluster: cortical-processing
  • FIO class: FIO-05 — Interpretation Failure
  • FIS criterion: FIS-5 — Interpretation

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Dorsal Stream is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-05 — Interpretation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon. The Focus Integrity Codes page explains how to read these codes.

Mechanism

The dorsal stream projects from primary visual cortex through parietal areas, carrying fast, motion-sensitive magnocellular input used to localize objects and guide movement.

Why It Matters

It supplies the visual control of action — reaching, grasping, and navigating — often without conscious awareness of the computations involved. In the thesis it is an interpretation route specialized for where things are and how to act on them.

Failure Mode

Parietal damage to the dorsal stream can impair reaching toward or navigating around objects that the person can still recognize, dissociating action from identity.

Human–Machine Bridge

Robotics and visual-SLAM systems implement a dorsal-like function, using vision for localization and action separately from the recognition that names what is seen.

Common Misunderstanding

The dorsal stream is often labeled simply the where pathway; it is better understood as the how pathway, computing vision for action rather than mere location.

Source Notes

The pathway and its lesion effects rest on neuroscience references (SRC-009, SRC-013). The reading of the dorsal stream as an action-oriented interpretation route is internal Zonules.com framing (SRC-004).
