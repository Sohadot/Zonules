Ensemble Perception

The crowd summarized before any one face is read.

Definition

Ensemble perception is the visual system's ability to summarize sets of similar items by their statistics, such as average size or orientation.

Scientific Grounding

Observers can report the mean of a group accurately even when they cannot report any individual member. This summary representation provides a compact description of crowded or cluttered scenes.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Ensemble perception is a separation-layer compression — it extracts stable structure from many noisy elements.
