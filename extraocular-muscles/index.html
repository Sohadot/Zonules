Extraocular Muscles

The actuators that aim the eye wherever attention demands.

Definition

The extraocular muscles are the six muscles that rotate each eye within the orbit to direct gaze.

Scientific Grounding

They comprise four rectus and two oblique muscles, each producing characteristic eye rotations. Coordinated action of the extraocular muscles of both eyes maintains binocular alignment.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

The extraocular muscles are the actuators of the accommodation-of-gaze system (FIO-02): the mechanism that physically re-aims focus.
