Ocular Fixation

Holding still is not stillness—it is active stabilization.

Definition

Ocular fixation is the maintenance of gaze on a single location, holding the target's image on the fovea.

Scientific Grounding

Even during fixation the eyes make tiny involuntary movements: microsaccades, drift, and tremor. These fixational eye movements prevent the perceptual fading of a stabilized image.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Fixation is the ocular suspension of focus (FIO-01): active stabilization that holds the focal target in place.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Ocular Fixation is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-01 — Suspension concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
