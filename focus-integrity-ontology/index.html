The Focus Integrity Ontology

This is the governing spine of the asset. The Focus Integrity Standard defines what intact focus is; the Focus Integrity Ontology classifies how it fails. The same five-part structure runs through anatomy, perception, and machine vision — because the logic of held focus is one logic across all three.

Every reference unit is tagged with an FIO class and an FIS criterion from this page. If you are meeting these codes for the first time, the Focus Integrity Codes page explains what they are and how to read them.

The Focus Integrity Standard — what intact focus is

A seeing system has focus integrity when all five criteria hold (FIS v0.1):

  • FIS-1 · Suspension. The structures holding the focal element are identified and stable.
  • FIS-2 · Accommodation. The system adjusts focus across its intended operating range.
  • FIS-3 · Separation. The system separates signal from noise within stated tolerances.
  • FIS-4 · Provenance. The system can establish the origin or authenticity of what it sees to a stated confidence.
  • FIS-5 · Interpretation. Held, clean focus yields correct interpretation, not merely sharp signal.

The Focus Integrity Ontology — how focus fails

Each failure class is the inverse of one standard criterion (FIO v0.1). Every class resolves to the governed reference units that explain it.

FIO-01 · Suspension Failure Inverse of FIS-1 · Suspension

The holding structures that keep the focal element in position themselves fail.

When the suspension layer fails, the focal element is present but unstable.
  • L1 Zonular weakness, dislocation, or rupture compromising lens position.
  • L2 Collapse of attentional anchoring; focal target cannot be held against competing stimuli.
  • L3 Loss of calibration or spatial grounding; the model's reference frame degrades.

FIO-02 · Accommodation Failure Inverse of FIS-2 · Accommodation

The system cannot adjust focus across its intended operating range.

A system that focuses at one setting only does not have focus integrity.
  • L1 Loss of accommodative range.
  • L2 Failure of perceptual constancy as conditions change.
  • L3 Domain shift; correct focus in training conditions, failure in new ones.

FIO-03 · Signal–Noise Failure Inverse of FIS-3 · Separation

The system cannot separate meaningful signal from noise.

Focus without separation produces sharp noise, not meaning.
  • L1 Optical scatter or media opacity reducing contrast at the receptor.
  • L2 Figure-ground collapse; salience misallocation.
  • L3 Adversarial perturbation, synthetic noise, or low signal-to-noise input.

FIO-04 · Provenance Failure Inverse of FIS-4 · Provenance

The system cannot establish whether what it sees is real or where it came from.

In an age of synthetic images, focus without provenance cannot yield visual truth.
  • L1 Conceptual bridge only — not strongly applicable at the anatomical layer.
  • L2 Misattributed source of a percept; false confidence in an illusion.
  • L3 Deepfake and synthetic media; absent or broken image provenance.

FIO-05 · Interpretation Failure Inverse of FIS-5 · Interpretation

Focus is held and signal is clean, but the resulting meaning is wrong.

The final failure of focus is not blur — it is confident misreading.
  • L1 Conceptual bridge only — interpretation is not an anatomical property.
  • L2 Perceptual error; correct sharp percept, incorrect meaning.
  • L3 Machine sight without machine understanding; correct detection, wrong interpretation.
