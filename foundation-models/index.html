Vision Foundation Models

A general visual base many tasks are built on.

Definition

Vision foundation models are large models pretrained on broad data that can be adapted to many downstream tasks.

Scientific Grounding

Pretraining at scale lets them transfer to new tasks with little task-specific data. The same broad pretraining that gives them generality can also propagate biases from the training data.

*Sources: SRC-019, SRC-022, SRC-017. See the source registry for classification.*

Machine-Vision Role

Foundation models are a suspension-layer base: they hold a general visual representation that many tasks are built upon.
