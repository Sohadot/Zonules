Generative Models

Generative models are a provenance failure at scale

Definition

A generative model is a statistical model that learns the joint distribution of inputs and can produce new samples indistinguishable from training data.

Scientific Grounding

Generative adversarial networks, variational autoencoders, and diffusion models are the primary architectures; diffusion models dominate recent image synthesis benchmarks. The near-perfect visual fidelity of outputs from modern generative models severs the assumed link between image appearance and image origin.

*Sources: SRC-007, SRC-008. See the source registry for classification.*

Technical Role

Generative models are a provenance failure at scale (FIO-04): they produce appearance without origin, making the appearance-provenance link impossible to assume.
