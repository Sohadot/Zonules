Geometric Illusions

Rules that recover real structure can misfire.

Definition

Geometric illusions are systematic misperceptions of the length, size, or angle of figures.

Scientific Grounding

In the Müller-Lyer illusion, identical lines appear to differ in length because of their end decorations. Many such illusions are linked to mechanisms that normally aid depth and size interpretation.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Geometric illusions are an interpretation-layer misfire: rules that usually recover real structure produce confident error.
