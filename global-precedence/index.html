Global Precedence

The forest is seen before the trees that compose it.

Definition

Global precedence is the tendency to perceive the overall form of a configuration before its local parts.

Scientific Grounding

In hierarchical figures, where a large letter is made of small different letters, the global letter is usually identified faster. Global structure can interfere with local judgments more than local structure interferes with global ones.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Global precedence is an interpretation-layer ordering — the whole is read before the parts.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Global Precedence is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-05 — Interpretation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
