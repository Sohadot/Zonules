Ground Truth

The agreed reality a system's claims are checked against.

Definition

Ground truth is the reference annotation against which a model's predictions are compared.

Scientific Grounding

It is usually produced by human labeling and is treated as the standard of correctness. Errors or bias in the ground truth propagate directly into measured performance and into the model.

*Sources: SRC-022, SRC-017. See the source registry for classification.*

Machine-Vision Role

Ground truth is a provenance-layer anchor: it is the agreed reality a system's claims are checked against.
