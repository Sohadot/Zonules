Keypoint Detection

Distinctive points a system can always find again.

Definition

Keypoint detection finds distinctive, repeatable points in an image, such as corners and blobs, that can be reliably located again.

Scientific Grounding

Good keypoints are invariant to scale and rotation and stable under moderate viewpoint and lighting change. They anchor tasks ranging from image alignment to pose estimation.

*Sources: SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

Machine-Vision Role

Keypoints are suspension-layer anchors — fixed reference points that hold a spatial frame steady.
