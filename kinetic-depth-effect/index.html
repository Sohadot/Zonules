Kinetic Depth Effect

Depth recovered from changing two-dimensional motion.

Definition

The kinetic depth effect is the perception of three-dimensional structure from the two-dimensional motion of a rotating object's projection.

Scientific Grounding

A shadow of a bent wire figure looks flat when still but appears solid once it rotates. The effect shows that motion alone can specify depth.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

The kinetic depth effect is an interpretation-layer recovery: depth read out of changing two-dimensional motion.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Kinetic Depth Effect is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-05 — Interpretation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
