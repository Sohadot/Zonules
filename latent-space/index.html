Latent Space

A model's hidden coordinate frame for images.

Definition

A latent space is the learned, lower-dimensional space in which a model represents the essential factors of its data.

Scientific Grounding

Nearby points in a well-structured latent space decode to similar images, and directions can correspond to interpretable attributes. Generative models create new images by sampling points in the latent space and decoding them.

*Sources: SRC-017. See the source registry for classification.*

Machine-Vision Role

Latent space is a suspension-layer scaffold: it is the hidden coordinate frame that holds a model's sense of what images are.
