Light Adaptation

Light adaptation is an accommodation mechanism

Definition

Light adaptation is the process by which the visual system reduces its sensitivity and adjusts its operating range in response to increased illumination.

Scientific Grounding

Multiple mechanisms contribute, including pupillary constriction, photoreceptor pigment bleaching, and neural gain control. Light adaptation allows the visual system to operate across a range of light intensities exceeding a factor of a billion.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Light adaptation is an accommodation mechanism (FIO-02): it recalibrates the system's input range across luminance environments without changing optical geometry.
