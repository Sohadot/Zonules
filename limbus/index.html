Limbus

The border where the clear window meets the white wall.

Definition

The limbus is the transitional border zone where the transparent cornea meets the opaque sclera and conjunctiva.

Scientific Grounding

It houses the limbal stem cells that continuously renew the corneal epithelium. The limbus also contains the drainage structures at the angle through which aqueous humor leaves the eye.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

The limbus is a structural boundary that maintains the cornea's renewal and the eye's fluid balance — a suspension-layer maintenance zone.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Limbus is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-01 — Suspension concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
