Mach Bands

Edge-enhancing machinery invents contrast.

Definition

Mach bands are illusory bright and dark bands seen at the edges of a luminance gradient.

Scientific Grounding

They appear where the gradient changes slope, exaggerating the transition. The effect is attributed to lateral inhibition in early visual processing.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Mach bands are a separation-layer artifact: edge-enhancing machinery invents contrast the signal does not contain.

  • Layer: L2 — Perception

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Mach Bands is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-03 — Separation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
