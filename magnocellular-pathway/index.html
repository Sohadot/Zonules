Magnocellular Pathway

The fast stream tuned to where and when.

Definition

The magnocellular pathway is a visual stream, originating in parasol ganglion cells, specialized for motion and coarse spatial detail.

Scientific Grounding

It conducts signals rapidly and is highly sensitive to low contrast but largely color-blind. It occupies the two ventral magnocellular layers of the lateral geniculate nucleus.

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

The magnocellular pathway is a separation-layer channel tuned to where and when, not what.
