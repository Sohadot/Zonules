Mental Imagery

Mental imagery is an interpretation of the absence of signal

Definition

Mental imagery is the experience of visual representations in the absence of corresponding external stimulation.

Scientific Grounding

Mental imagery activates overlapping cortical regions with direct visual perception, including early visual areas, though with lower fidelity and resolution. The ability to generate and manipulate internal visual representations varies substantially across individuals, from vivid imagery to near-absence (aphantasia).

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Mental imagery is an interpretation of the absence of signal (FIO-05): the visual system constructs a representation from internal knowledge rather than incoming sensory data.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Mental Imagery is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-05 — Interpretation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
