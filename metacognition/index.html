Metacognition

Metacognition is an interpretation of one's own interpretations

Definition

Metacognition is the capacity to monitor and evaluate one's own cognitive processes, including assessing the confidence and accuracy of one's own perceptual judgments.

Scientific Grounding

Perceptual metacognition is separable from perceptual sensitivity: observers can be accurate in their judgments but poorly calibrated in their confidence, or vice versa. Poor metacognitive accuracy has been linked to deficits in prefrontal function, demonstrating a dissociation between first-order perception and its monitoring.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Metacognition is an interpretation of one's own interpretations (FIO-05): the confidence signal that guides behavior is itself a constructed output that can fail independently of the primary perceptual judgment.
