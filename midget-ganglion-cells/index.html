Midget Ganglion Cells

The retina's channel for fine detail and color.

Definition

Midget ganglion cells are small retinal ganglion cells that dominate the fovea and carry fine spatial and color detail.

Scientific Grounding

Each foveal midget cell can receive input from a single cone, giving very high spatial resolution. They feed the parvocellular pathway to the lateral geniculate nucleus.

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Midget cells are a separation-layer channel for detail: they resolve the finest spatial and chromatic signal the retina sends on.
