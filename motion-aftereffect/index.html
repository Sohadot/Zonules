Motion Aftereffect

Stare at a waterfall, then watch the rocks drift up.

Definition

The motion aftereffect is the illusion of motion in the opposite direction seen after prolonged viewing of steady motion.

Scientific Grounding

Adapting to downward motion, for example, makes a stationary scene appear to drift upward. The effect reflects adaptation in direction-selective motion detectors that become temporarily imbalanced.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

The motion aftereffect is an accommodation-layer cost — recalibration to one condition distorts the reading of the next.
