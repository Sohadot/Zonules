Motion Parallax

Move your head and the near rushes past the far.

Definition

Motion parallax is the depth cue that arises when the observer moves and nearer objects sweep across the retina faster than farther ones.

Scientific Grounding

The relative image speed of objects provides information about their relative distance. It is a monocular cue, available to a single moving eye without binocular input.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Motion parallax is interpretation-layer evidence — depth read out of the structure of changing motion.
