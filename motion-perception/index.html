Motion Perception

Movement is inferred, not directly sampled.

Definition

Motion perception is the process by which the visual system detects and interprets movement in the visual field.

Scientific Grounding

Specialized mechanisms respond selectively to the direction and speed of moving stimuli. Motion can be perceived even from static or intermittent stimuli under certain conditions.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Motion perception is an interpretation process (FIO-05): movement is inferred from sampled signals rather than read off directly.
