Nystagmus

Nystagmus is an accommodation failure

Definition

Nystagmus is an involuntary, rhythmic oscillation of the eyes that impairs fixation stability and is the most common oculomotor disorder.

Scientific Grounding

It may be congenital or acquired; acquired nystagmus commonly results from lesions in the brainstem, cerebellum, or vestibular pathways. The slow drift is the failure of gaze stabilization; the fast corrective saccade is the compensatory reflex.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Nystagmus is an accommodation failure (FIO-02): the oculomotor system fails to maintain stable gaze, and the fixation point drifts continuously rather than holding.
