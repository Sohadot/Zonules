Ocular Dominance Columns

Where the two eyes' signals come into register.

Definition

Ocular dominance columns are alternating bands of visual cortex that respond preferentially to input from one eye or the other.

Scientific Grounding

Hubel and Wiesel mapped these columns and showed they reorganize if normal binocular input is disrupted during development. They provide the cortical substrate where the two eyes' signals are brought together.

*Sources: SRC-010, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Ocular dominance columns are a suspension-layer structure: they hold the two monocular signals in the alignment binocular vision requires.
