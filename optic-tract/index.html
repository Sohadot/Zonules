Optic Tract

A single non-redundant conduit carrying half the visual world from the chiasm toward the cortex.

Definition

Each optic tract carries post-chiasmatic visual fibres from the ipsilateral temporal retina and the contralateral nasal retina, representing the contralateral visual field, from the optic chiasm to the lateral geniculate nucleus.

Scientific Grounding

The retinotopic organisation of the visual field is preserved through the optic tract: neighbouring fibres carry signals from neighbouring points in the visual field.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-002. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

The optic tract is a suspension element of the visual pathway (FIO-01): a non-redundant structural conduit whose failure collapses signal delivery to the cortex.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Optic Tract is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-01 (Suspension) concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
