Optical Aberration

Every real optical system adds its own noise.

Definition

Optical aberrations are imperfections in an optical system that prevent light from converging to a single ideal point.

Scientific Grounding

The eye exhibits both lower-order aberrations, such as defocus, and higher-order aberrations that degrade fine image quality. Aberrations reduce retinal image contrast even when the gross focus is correct.

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Optical aberration is a separation-layer limit: it is the residual noise an imperfect optical system adds to every signal it forms.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Optical Aberration is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-03 — Separation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
