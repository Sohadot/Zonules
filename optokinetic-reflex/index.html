Optokinetic Reflex

The optokinetic reflex is a gaze suspension mechanism

Definition

The optokinetic reflex is a reflexive eye movement that stabilizes the visual image during sustained whole-field motion by rotating the eyes in the direction of motion.

Scientific Grounding

The reflex is driven by the direction of global visual motion and is mediated by subcortical pathways distinct from those driving voluntary smooth pursuit. Like the vestibulo-ocular reflex, it operates with a slow phase in the direction of motion and a fast corrective saccade in the opposite direction.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

The optokinetic reflex is a gaze suspension mechanism (FIO-02): it holds the image stable on the retina during environmental motion, preventing global retinal slip from destroying acuity.
