Overt Attention

Focus held by aiming the eye's sharpest region.

Definition

Overt attention is the selection of a location or object accompanied by movement of the eyes to fixate it.

Scientific Grounding

Most everyday viewing uses overt shifts, moving the high-resolution fovea to points of interest. Eye-movement records are widely used to infer where overt attention is directed.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Overt attention is a suspension-layer act: it holds focus by aiming the eye's sharpest region at the target.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Overt Attention is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-01 — Suspension concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
