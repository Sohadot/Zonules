Parasol Ganglion Cells

The retina's channel for motion and contrast.

Definition

Parasol ganglion cells are large retinal ganglion cells with wide dendritic fields that pool input from many photoreceptors.

Scientific Grounding

They respond quickly and transiently and are sensitive to motion and low-contrast luminance changes. They feed the magnocellular pathway to the lateral geniculate nucleus.

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Parasol cells are a separation-layer channel for change: they extract motion and contrast from the retinal signal.
