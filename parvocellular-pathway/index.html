Parvocellular Pathway

The detailed stream tuned to what.

Definition

The parvocellular pathway is a visual stream, originating in midget ganglion cells, specialized for fine detail and color.

Scientific Grounding

It conducts signals more slowly and sustains its response, supporting high spatial resolution. It occupies the four dorsal parvocellular layers of the lateral geniculate nucleus.

*Sources: SRC-009, SRC-013. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

The parvocellular pathway is a separation-layer channel tuned to what: fine, colored, detailed signal.
