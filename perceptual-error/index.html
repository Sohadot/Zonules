Perceptual Error

Perceptual error is the visible output of interpretation failure: a clear signal given the wrong meaning.

Definition

Perceptual errors arise from systematic biases in sensory processing, context effects, prior expectations, and limits of neural computation, and are distinct from sensory noise.

Scientific Grounding

Optical illusions are canonical demonstrations of perceptual error, where the visual system's heuristics for interpreting depth, size, and brightness produce demonstrably incorrect interpretations. Perceptual errors are not random failures but often the predictable output of adaptively useful heuristics operating outside their domain of validity.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, perceptual error is the observable output of FIO-05 Interpretation failure: the point where a well-transmitted signal is assigned an incorrect meaning.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Perceptual Error is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-05 (Interpretation) concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.

Mechanism

Perceptual errors arise when the heuristics that normally recover the world from ambiguous input are applied to input that violates their assumptions, yielding a confident but wrong percept.

Why It Matters

Errors are not noise to be dismissed; they are the clearest evidence of how perception works, revealing the inferences the system makes by showing where they break. Perceptual error is the canonical interpretation failure: focus is held and the signal is clean, yet the meaning is wrong.

Human–Machine Bridge

Machine perception shows the same signature in adversarial examples and shortcut learning: a sharp input read with confident, incorrect meaning. Sharing the FIO-05 class lets one standard ask of a person and a model whether clean focus produced the correct reading.

Common Misunderstanding

A perceptual error is often taken to mean poor eyesight; in fact illusions fool observers with perfect optics, because the error is in interpretation, not in the signal.
