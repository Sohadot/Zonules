Perceptual Learning

Practice retunes the senses, not just the response.

Definition

Perceptual learning is the lasting improvement in perceptual ability that follows practice with a sensory task.

Scientific Grounding

Training can sharpen discriminations such as fine orientation or texture differences, sometimes specific to the trained location or feature. The improvements reflect changes in how sensory information is weighted and read out, not changes in the stimulus.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-009. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Perceptual learning is an accommodation-layer change — the system retunes its range to the demands it has met.
