Perceptual Load

Attention has an aperture, and how much the scene demands decides how wide it can open.

Definition

Perceptual load theory proposes that high-load tasks exhaust limited attentional capacity, suppressing processing of task-irrelevant stimuli automatically.

Scientific Grounding

Under low perceptual load, spare cognitive capacity is allocated to distractors, increasing susceptibility to irrelevant stimulation. Perceptual load interacts with selective attention, with high load producing more focused, effortless exclusion of competing visual information than low load conditions.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, perceptual load maps to FIO-02 Accommodation: it defines the effective aperture of attentional focus available to any given visual scene.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Perceptual Load is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-02 (Accommodation) concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
