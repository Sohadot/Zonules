Photopic Vision

Photopic vision is the high-signal operating mode of the visual system

Definition

Photopic vision is the visual function of the eye under well-lit conditions, mediated by cone photoreceptors.

Scientific Grounding

Cones are concentrated in and around the fovea; the three cone types (S, M, L) underlie trichromatic color vision. Photopic vision has high spatial acuity and color discrimination but lower sensitivity than scotopic vision.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Photopic vision is the high-signal operating mode of the visual system (FIO-03): it maximizes signal resolution and color discrimination at the cost of absolute sensitivity.
