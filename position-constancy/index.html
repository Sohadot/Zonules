Position Constancy

The scene held still against the motion we add.

Definition

Position constancy is the perception of a stable world despite the constant motion of the retinal image caused by eye movements.

Scientific Grounding

The visual system uses information about its own eye movements to discount the resulting image motion. When this compensation fails, the world appears to move with the eyes.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Position constancy is an accommodation-layer correction: the scene is held still against the motion the observer adds.

Layer Classification

  • Layer: L2 — Perception

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Position Constancy is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-02 — Accommodation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
