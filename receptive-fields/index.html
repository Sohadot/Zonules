Receptive Fields

Receptive fields are the signal boundaries of the visual system

Definition

A receptive field is the region of sensory space within which a stimulus activates a given sensory neuron.

Scientific Grounding

Ganglion cells, LGN neurons, and cortical cells each have characteristic receptive field sizes and response properties that define their sensitivity to contrast and edges. Center-surround organization in retinal ganglion cells creates spatial antagonism that enhances edges and suppresses uniform illumination.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Receptive fields are the signal boundaries of the visual system (FIO-03): they define what can be distinguished from what and where the signal-noise threshold is set.
