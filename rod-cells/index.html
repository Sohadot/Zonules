Rod Cells

The photoreceptors that see in near-darkness.

Definition

Rod cells are the photoreceptors responsible for vision in dim light, far more numerous than cones in the human retina.

Scientific Grounding

A single rod can respond to an individual photon, giving the rod system extreme sensitivity. Rods are absent from the foveola and reach their highest density in a ring in the peripheral retina.

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-012. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Rods are a separation-layer instrument tuned for low signal: they pull a usable percept out of near-darkness.
