Saliency Maps

A picture of where a model claims it looked.

Definition

Saliency maps are visualizations that estimate how much each input pixel influenced a model's output.

Scientific Grounding

They are computed from gradients or related signals to highlight the regions a network appears to rely on. Saliency maps can mislead, sometimes looking plausible even when they do not faithfully reflect the model's reasoning.

*Sources: SRC-017. See the source registry for classification.*

Machine-Vision Role

Saliency maps are an interpretation-layer probe — an attempt to check whether a model focused on the right evidence.
