Scotoma

A scotoma is a provenance gap

Definition

A scotoma is a region of absent or diminished vision within the visual field surrounded by areas of normal or relatively normal vision.

Scientific Grounding

Scotomas arise from damage to retinal tissue, the optic nerve, or higher visual pathways; their shape and location map to the anatomical site of the lesion. The physiological scotoma at the optic disc is not perceived because the visual system fills it in using surrounding information — the same process as the blind spot.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

A scotoma is a provenance gap (FIO-04): the visual system receives no signal from a region of the world yet may not register its own absence, making the gap invisible as a gap.
