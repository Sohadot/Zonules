Shape Constancy

A door is a rectangle from every angle it is seen.

Definition

Shape constancy is the perception of an object's stable shape despite changes in the shape of its retinal projection with viewing angle.

Scientific Grounding

A door swinging open projects a series of trapezoids, yet it is seen as a constant rectangle. The visual system uses slant and depth information to recover an object's true shape from its projection.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Shape constancy is an accommodation-layer property — interpretation stays stable across the range of viewpoints.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Shape Constancy is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-02 — Accommodation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
