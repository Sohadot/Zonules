Simultaneous Contrast

A region is read against its surround, not alone.

Definition

Simultaneous contrast is the shift in a region's apparent brightness or color caused by its surroundings.

Scientific Grounding

An identical gray patch looks lighter on a dark background and darker on a light one. The effect reflects lateral interactions that emphasize the differences between adjacent regions.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Simultaneous contrast is a separation-layer bias: the system reads a region against its surround, not in absolute terms.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Simultaneous Contrast is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-03 — Separation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
