Attention Capture

A salient distractor is noise that competes with the signal for the same limited attention.

Definition

Attention capture is the involuntary reorientation of visual attention driven by stimulus salience — features such as abrupt onset, high contrast, or sudden motion automatically recruit attention.

Scientific Grounding

The distinction between goal-directed (endogenous) and stimulus-driven (exogenous) attention determines whether attentional shifts are voluntary or reflexively triggered by environmental events. Salient distractors can capture attention even when observers are explicitly instructed to ignore them, demonstrating the automaticity of certain capture mechanisms.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, attention capture is classified under FIO-03 Signal-Noise Separation: a salient but irrelevant stimulus generates noise that competes with the target signal for attentional resources.
