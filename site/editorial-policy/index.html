Editorial Policy

A reference corpus is not a content library. Every unit must earn its position and hold it.

Why this policy exists

On a governed reference asset, editorial decisions are governance decisions. What gets added, how it is maintained, and what gets removed are not matters of convenience or traffic optimization. They follow rules, and those rules are documented here.

The entry rule

A reference unit may only be added to this asset if it meets all of the following conditions:

  • It is relevant to the governing thesis — that focus is produced by hidden structural tension — at Layer 01 (anatomy), Layer 02 (perception), or Layer 03 (machine vision and verification).
  • It has at least one registered claim with a sourced status, resting on a registered external reference.
  • It has no unsourced scientific assertion in its body.
  • It carries the appropriate safety classification and the safety framing that classification requires.
  • It is registered in the route registry before publication.
  • It passes the blocking quality gate without exception.

A unit that meets five of these six conditions is not published. Every condition is required.

Conceptual content

Framework-level claims — those mapping the governing thesis across layers — are permitted and clearly labelled as internal conceptual framework language, not as external scientific fact. No conceptual mapping may be presented as evidence. The FIO and FIS structures are the governed framework for these mappings; they are versioned, append-only, and documented.

Maintenance

Every reference unit records a `last_reviewed` date. Content is reviewed when the governing registered source is updated, when the scientific consensus it depends on shifts, or on a periodic governance cycle. A unit with an outdated review date is flagged before the next publication cycle.

No content is silently modified. Changes that alter the substance of a registered claim require a new claim entry; changes that alter the FIO classification of a unit require an entry in the decision log.

Removal

A unit is removed if:

  • The source it depends on has been retracted or superseded and no adequate replacement exists.
  • The governing thesis no longer supports the unit's layer assignment.
  • The unit cannot pass the quality gate.

Removal is recorded in the decision log with the reason. A removed unit's route is marked `status: retired` in the registry; its path is not reused.

Out-of-scope content

The following are not part of this asset and will not be added:

  • Clinical guidance, diagnosis, treatment recommendations, or anything that could be read as medical advice.
  • Price, valuation, or transaction content (governed separately by the Acquisition Posture).
  • Opinion, commentary, or content without a registered claim basis.
  • Thin pages created for search performance rather than reference value.

Notes

This policy is enforced by the blocking quality gate, not by manual review alone. See the methodology for the governance architecture and the source policy for how claims and sources are registered.
