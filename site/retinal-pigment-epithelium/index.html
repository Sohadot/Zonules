Retinal Pigment Epithelium

Invisible maintenance that keeps the photoreceptors working day after day.

Definition

The retinal pigment epithelium (RPE) is a single layer of pigmented cells between the photoreceptors and the choroid that supports photoreceptor function.

Scientific Grounding

The RPE recycles visual pigment, phagocytoses shed photoreceptor outer segments, and absorbs stray light. RPE dysfunction is implicated in retinal degenerations such as age-related macular degeneration.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*
