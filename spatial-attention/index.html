Spatial Attention

Spatial attention is a suspension mechanism

Definition

Spatial attention is the selective enhancement of processing for stimuli at a particular location in the visual field, operating like a spotlight that brightens one region of the scene.

Scientific Grounding

Attending to a location increases the apparent contrast of stimuli there, speeds detection responses, and reduces the contrast threshold for target detection. Spatial attention can be covertly directed to locations other than the point of fixation, demonstrating that the locus of attention and the locus of gaze are distinct.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Spatial attention is a suspension mechanism (FIO-01): it holds a selected region of the visual scene in enhanced processing, maintaining the focus of analysis independent of the direction of gaze.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Spatial Attention is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-01 — Suspension concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
