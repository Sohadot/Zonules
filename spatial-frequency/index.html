Spatial Frequency

Spatial frequency is a signal decomposition axis

Definition

Spatial frequency describes the periodicity of luminance variation across space, measured in cycles per degree of visual angle, and is a fundamental description of the visual signal.

Scientific Grounding

The visual system analyzes the image through channels tuned to different spatial frequencies: low frequencies carry coarse shape and layout; high frequencies carry fine detail and edges. The contrast sensitivity function describes the visual system's sensitivity at each spatial frequency, peaking at intermediate frequencies and falling off at high and low extremes.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Spatial frequency is a signal decomposition axis (FIO-03): the visual system reads the world as a multi-channel signal in which information at different scales is separated before being integrated.
