Spherical Aberration

The aperture that gathers more light also blurs it.

Definition

Spherical aberration occurs when light passing through the periphery of a lens focuses at a different point than light through its center.

Scientific Grounding

A larger pupil admits more peripheral rays and therefore increases spherical aberration. The cornea and lens of the eye partly offset each other's spherical aberration.

*Sources: SRC-011, SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

Anatomical Role

Spherical aberration is a separation-layer trade-off: the same aperture that gathers more light also degrades its focus.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Spherical Aberration is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-03 — Separation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
