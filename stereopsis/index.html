Stereopsis

Stereopsis is an interpretation

Definition

Stereopsis is the perception of depth produced by the visual system's comparison of the slightly different images received by each eye.

Scientific Grounding

The horizontal disparity between corresponding retinal images is the primary binocular cue to relative depth and is processed by specialized neurons in visual cortex. Stereopsis requires precise binocular alignment; misalignment such as strabismus disrupts the disparity signal and can prevent its development.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Stereopsis is an interpretation (FIO-05): the third dimension of space is not given in the optical input but constructed by the visual system from a binocular difference signal.
