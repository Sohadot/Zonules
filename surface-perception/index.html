Surface Perception

Light organized into the surfaces of a scene.

Definition

Surface perception is the recovery of the surfaces in a scene, including their slant, boundaries, and continuity.

Scientific Grounding

The visual system groups image regions into surfaces and assigns them a depth order. Surfaces, not raw points of light, are treated as the basic units of many later perceptual judgments.

*Sources: SRC-006, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Surface perception is an interpretation-layer structure: it organizes light into the surfaces a scene is made of.
