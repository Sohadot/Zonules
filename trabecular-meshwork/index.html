Trabecular Meshwork

The drain that keeps the eye's internal pressure—and its shape—stable.

Definition

The trabecular meshwork is the spongy tissue at the iridocorneal angle through which aqueous humor drains from the eye.

Scientific Grounding

It regulates aqueous outflow and thereby helps maintain intraocular pressure. Impaired outflow through the trabecular meshwork is a major mechanism of open-angle glaucoma.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

The trabecular meshwork is a pressure-suspension element (FIO-01): hidden regulation that keeps the globe's geometry stable.
