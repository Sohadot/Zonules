Troxler Fading

A signal that never changes becomes no signal.

Definition

Troxler fading is the disappearance of a steady peripheral stimulus during prolonged fixation.

Scientific Grounding

It occurs because unchanging input on stable retinal locations is adapted away. Small eye movements normally refresh the image and prevent such fading.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-014. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Troxler fading is an accommodation-layer side effect: a signal that never changes is treated as no signal at all.

Layer Classification

  • Layer: L2 — Perception

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Troxler Fading is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as an FIO-02 — Accommodation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
