Vergence

Vergence is an accommodation mechanism

Definition

Vergence refers to the disjunctive movements of the two eyes that maintain alignment of the visual axes on objects at different distances.

Scientific Grounding

Convergence and divergence are driven by disparity signals from corresponding retinal points and by accommodative demand. Vergence and accommodation are neurally coupled: the vergence-accommodation reflex links the focusing effort of the lens to the binocular alignment of the eyes.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-003. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Vergence is an accommodation mechanism (FIO-02): it maintains the geometric alignment required for stereopsis and binocular single vision as viewing distance changes.
