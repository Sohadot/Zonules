Viewpoint Invariance

Identity held stable across the angles a thing is seen.

Definition

Viewpoint invariance is the ability to recognize an object across changes in viewing angle.

Scientific Grounding

Recognition is often slower for unusual or unfamiliar viewpoints, suggesting partial rather than complete invariance. Theories differ on whether recognition stores multiple views or recovers a viewpoint-independent structure.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-015. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Viewpoint invariance is an interpretation-layer constancy: identity held stable across the angles a thing is seen from.
