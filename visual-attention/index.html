Visual Attention

Visual attention is the suspension mechanism of perception

Definition

Visual attention is the selective processing of part of the visual scene at the expense of other parts, determining what receives deeper analysis and reaches awareness.

Scientific Grounding

Attention can be directed by stimulus salience (bottom-up) or by goals and expectations (top-down); the relative influence depends on task demands and sensory context. Both spatial attention and feature-based attention modulate activity in early visual cortex, amplifying signals from attended locations or features.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Visual attention is the suspension mechanism of perception (FIO-01): it holds selected parts of the visual scene in the state required for detailed analysis — the cognitive analogue of zonular tension.
