Visual Illusions

When the system's inferences override the signal.

Definition

Visual illusions are systematic discrepancies between the perceived and physical properties of a stimulus.

Scientific Grounding

They reveal the constructive assumptions the visual system makes about the world. Many illusions persist even when the viewer knows the true nature of the stimulus.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Visual illusions are interpretation errors (FIO-05): the system's inferences override the available signal.

Layer Classification

  • Layer: L2 — Perception
  • Cluster: perceptual-clarity

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Visual Illusions is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-05 — Interpretation concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
