Visual Masking

A present signal can be erased by competing input around it.

Definition

Visual masking is the reduction or elimination of the visibility of one stimulus by another presented in close spatial or temporal proximity.

Scientific Grounding

Masking can occur when the mask precedes the target (forward masking) or follows it (backward masking). Masking is widely used to study the time course of visual processing.

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

Visual masking is a signal-noise failure (FIO-03): a present signal is suppressed by competing input.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Visual Masking is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-03 — Signal-Noise concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
