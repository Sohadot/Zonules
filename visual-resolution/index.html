Visual Resolution

Visual resolution is a signal discrimination limit

Definition

Visual resolution is the ability of the eye to distinguish fine detail, measured as the minimum angular separation of two points perceived as distinct.

Scientific Grounding

Maximum visual resolution occurs at the fovea, where cone density is highest; resolution falls steeply with eccentricity. Clinical visual acuity (Snellen chart) measures high-contrast resolution and may understate the impact of conditions that primarily reduce contrast sensitivity.

*Sources: SRC-001, SRC-005. See the source registry for classification.*

Structural Role

Visual resolution is a signal discrimination limit (FIO-03): it defines the spatial grain of the signal channel and determines what spatial information can enter the system at all.
