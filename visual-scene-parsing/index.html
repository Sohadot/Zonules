Visual Scene Parsing

Assigning structure and meaning to a scene introduces interpretation risk even when the optics are clean.

Definition

Visual scene parsing involves simultaneous segmentation of figure from ground, grouping of elements into objects, and assignment of spatial relationships between objects.

Scientific Grounding

Gist perception — the rapid, holistic extraction of a scene's category and dominant properties within the first 100 ms of viewing — precedes detailed object-level parsing. Scene parsing is influenced by both bottom-up salience (contrast, motion, novelty) and top-down knowledge (scene schemas, expected object locations).

*Sources: SRC-005, SRC-006. See the source registry for classification.*

Perceptual Role

In the Zonules FIO framework, visual scene parsing is classified under FIO-05 Interpretation: the act of assigning meaning and structure to a visual scene introduces interpretation-layer risk even when the optical signal is clean.
