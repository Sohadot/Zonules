Visual Tracking

Identity and position held across frames.

Definition

Visual tracking is the machine vision task of following the position of one or more targets across video frames.

Scientific Grounding

Trackers must handle appearance change, occlusion, and motion of the target. Errors accumulate when a target is lost and then re-acquired incorrectly.

*Sources: SRC-007. See the source registry for classification.*

Machine-Vision Role

Visual tracking is a suspension over time (FIO-01): target identity and position are held across frames.

Relationship to the Governing Thesis

Visual Tracking is one instance of the asset's governing claim: that focus is produced and held by structure that is not itself visible in the result. Its place in the focus-integrity ontology is fixed as a FIO-01 — Suspension concern, the same failure class whether the seeing system is made of tissue, attention, or silicon.
